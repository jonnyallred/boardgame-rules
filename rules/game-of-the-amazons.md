## Overview

The Game of the Amazons is a two-player abstract strategy game on a 10x10 board. Each player controls four amazons. On each turn, a player moves one amazon (like a chess queen) and then shoots an arrow from its landing square (also queen-like movement) to permanently block a square. The board progressively fills with arrows, restricting movement. The last player able to make a legal move wins. Draws are impossible.

## Components

- 1 game board (10x10 grid)
- 4 White amazons
- 4 Black amazons
- 90 red counters (arrows)

## Setup

Place the 8 amazons on the board in the starting configuration:
- **White amazons:** a4, d1, g1, j4
- **Black amazons:** a7, d10, g10, j7

(White occupies the bottom two rows' edges; Black mirrors on the top.)

## Turn Structure

White moves first. Players alternate turns. Each turn consists of exactly two parts performed in sequence:

1. **Move an Amazon** — Move one of your own amazons any number of empty squares in a straight line (orthogonally or diagonally), exactly as a queen moves in chess. The amazon may not cross or enter any square occupied by an amazon of either color or by an arrow.

2. **Shoot an Arrow** — From the amazon's new position, shoot an arrow to another square using the same queen-like movement. The arrow may travel in any direction (including back along the path the amazon just traveled, into or across the starting square). The arrow cannot cross or enter a square occupied by another arrow or any amazon. Mark the arrow's landing square with a red counter — this square is permanently blocked.

## Actions

There is only one type of action: move an amazon, then shoot an arrow. Both parts are mandatory every turn.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The last player able to make a legal move wins. A player who cannot move any of their amazons on their turn loses. Draws are impossible in this game.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- An amazon may move back through its own starting square on the same turn (the starting square is vacated when the amazon moves).
- The arrow shot after moving may travel back along the exact path the amazon just took, including through or to the amazon's original square.
- Arrows are permanent — once placed, a red counter cannot be moved or removed.
- Both the amazon movement and arrow shot must follow unobstructed straight lines. Neither can jump over or land on occupied squares.
- There are no captures — amazons cannot take other amazons.
- The game always ends in a decisive result; draws cannot occur.

## Player Reference

**Turn Sequence:**
1. Move one of your 4 amazons (queen-like movement along empty squares)
2. Shoot an arrow from the amazon's new position (queen-like movement to an empty square)
3. Place a red counter on the arrow's landing square (permanently blocked)

**Movement Rules:**
- Orthogonal or diagonal, any number of squares
- Cannot cross or land on: other amazons (either color) or arrows
- Amazon movement and arrow shot are independent directions

**Board:** 10x10 grid, starting with 8 amazons placed symmetrically
