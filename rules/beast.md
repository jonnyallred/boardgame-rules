## Overview

Beast is an asymmetric hidden-movement game set in a dangerous Northern Expanse. One player controls a ferocious Beast, while the other players form a team of hunters. The Beast wins by killing enough settlers; the hunters win by killing the Beast or surviving until reinforcements arrive after enough rounds pass. The Beast moves secretly using face-down movement cards, while hunters move openly and must search to reveal the Beast's location before they can attack.

## Components

- 6 Beast character mats and 6 Hunter character mats
- 1 map (double-sided)
- 2 contracts
- 16 action cards (shared between Beast and Hunters)
- 24 Beast ability cards
- 48 Beast movement cards
- 26 item cards (Hunters only)
- 25 Beastly talent cards (Beast only)
- 18 Hunter ability cards
- 18 summons (Beast)
- 18 grudge tokens (6x value 3, 12x value 1)
- 14 upgrade markers
- 15 trail tokens
- 4 ancient power tokens
- 4 watchtower tokens
- 20 habitat tokens
- 7 farmer settlers, 4 noble settlers
- 28 sheep, 19 boars, 4 bears
- 15 wound tokens (5x value 3, 10x value 1)
- Character markers for all Beasts and Hunters

## Setup

1. **Supply:** Place habitat tokens, wound tokens, grudges, upgrade markers, watchtowers, and ancient powers in a central supply.
2. **Contract & Map:** Choose a contract and use the corresponding map side.
3. **Animals & Settlers:** Place animals and settlers on map locations specified by the contract.
4. **Beastly Talents:** Shuffle the deck, place 3 face-up beside it.
5. **Items:** Shuffle the item deck, place 3 face-up beside it.
6. **Action Cards:** Shuffle the action card deck.
7. **Beast Player:** Choose a Beast, take its ability cards and summons, place Beast marker at contract start location.
8. **Hunter Players:** Each choose a hunter, take their ability cards. In a 2-player game, the hunter player controls 2 hunters.
9. **Grudges & Trails:** Distribute grudges per contract setup. Place trail tokens near the Beast player.

## Turn Structure

The game is played over a series of rounds (days). Each round has 3 phases:

### Phase 1: Dawn (Drafting)
- Action cards are drafted between the Beast and hunters.
- **2 players:** Special 2-player draft rules apply.
- **3 players:** Each player receives and passes cards.
- **4 players:** Standard draft.
- Unused action cards are discarded.

### Phase 2: Day (Actions)
Players take turns playing cards. On your turn, you may play up to **one card** with each of the two action symbols (up to 2 cards total per turn). Cards are played and resolved one at a time.

The Beast plays the bottom effects of action cards; hunters play the top effects.

Players may also **pass** or **flee** (hunters only -- move away from danger).

### Phase 3: Night
1. **Check contract rewards** -- evaluate if any contract objectives are met.
2. **Discard and reset** -- discard remaining cards, reset the market.
3. **Restore health** -- killed hunters are resurrected at a settlement.
4. **Upgrade** -- spend grudges to upgrade your character.

## Actions

### Beast Movement
- The Beast moves secretly. For each step, the Beast player selects a movement card and places it **face-down** in the active movement section.
- The Beast marker stays at its "last known location." The Beast's actual location follows the face-down movement trail.
- "Rush" action: move up to 3 steps.
- "No Movement" cards allow the Beast to stay in place (not allowed if sharing location with a hunter or settler).

### Hunter Movement & Trails
- Hunters move openly by moving their marker on the map.
- When a hunter moves through a location matching one of the Beast's active movement cards, the Beast must place a **trail token** there.
- Trails help hunters track the Beast.

### Searching
- Play a card with a search effect. If the searched location matches the Beast's actual location, the Beast is **revealed** (shows all active movement cards, discards them and trail tokens, moves marker to actual location).
- The Beast can only be attacked when **revealed**.

### Attacking
- Play a card with an "Attack" effect. Every attack deals **1 damage** (1 wound token).
- When wounds equal a character's starting health, they are dead.
- Hunters can only attack summons and a revealed Beast.
- The Beast can attack anything. The Beast is revealed whenever it attacks.
- **Ancient Power** tokens add +1 damage to a single attack.

### Summons (Beast only)
- The Beast can place summons (creature allies) on the map.
- Summons can attack animals, settlers, hunters, and watchtowers.
- To gain grudges from kills, the Beast must strike the final blow.

### Grudges
- Resource tokens used for upgrades and playing certain cards.
- Hunters gain grudges from contract rewards and card effects.
- The Beast gains grudges primarily from killing animals, settlers, or hunters.

### Upgrades (Night Phase)
- Spend grudges to purchase upgrade markers that enhance your character.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Victory is determined by the chosen contract:
- **Beast wins** when enough settlers are dead (as specified by the contract).
- **Hunters win** when the Beast is dead, or when enough days (rounds) have passed and reinforcements arrive.
- If the Beast dies, the game ends immediately and hunters win (unless the contract states otherwise).

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Killed hunters** are resurrected during the Night phase at any settlement. They lose all item cards and grudges. If the Beast killed them, the Beast gains 1 grudge and randomly takes one of the hunter's action cards.
- **Habitat tokens** modify terrain and may be prerequisites for certain Beast actions.
- **Watchtowers** provide vision advantages for hunters.
- **Reactions** are special abilities that can be triggered outside your turn.
- **Beastly talents** are special Beast-only cards acquired during the game.
- **Items** are special Hunter-only cards acquired during the game.
- Prerequisites on cards may require specific grudge counts, habitats, or zone locations.
