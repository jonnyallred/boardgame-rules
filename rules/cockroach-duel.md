## Overview

Cockroach Duel (Kakerlaken-Duell) is a two-player bluffing and deduction game from Drei Magier Spiele. Two cockroaches face off on a straight track, each trying to lure the opponent's cockroach to their side of the board. Players secretly choose which direction to move by placing tokens face-down, trying to outguess their opponent. The first player to lure the opposing cockroach to their end of the track wins.

## Components

- 1 game board with a straight track of spaces
- 2 cockroach figures (one per player)
- Movement tokens for each player (Left/Right or Forward/Backward indicators)
- Bluffing tokens

## Setup

1. Place the game board between the two players.
2. Place both cockroach figures in the center of the track.
3. Each player takes their set of movement/bluffing tokens.
4. Determine who goes first.

## Turn Structure

Each round is simultaneous:
1. Both players secretly select a movement token and place it face-down.
2. Both tokens are revealed simultaneously.
3. Move the cockroach(es) according to the result.

## Actions

### Select Token
- Each player secretly chooses one of their available tokens and places it face-down.
- Tokens indicate the direction you want the cockroach to move.

### Reveal and Resolve
- Both tokens are flipped simultaneously.
- The cockroach moves based on the combination of both players' choices.
- If both players pick the same direction, the cockroach moves that way.
- If players pick different directions, a different resolution occurs (depending on the specific token combination).

### Movement
- The cockroach moves one or more spaces along the track toward one player's end.
- The goal is to maneuver the cockroach toward your opponent's side.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The first player to lure one of the cockroaches all the way to the opponent's end of the track wins the game.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- The game is entirely about bluffing and reading your opponent — there is no randomness.
- Some token types may have special effects beyond basic directional movement.
- The game is very quick, making it suitable for best-of-three or best-of-five series.
- The track length determines game duration — shorter tracks lead to faster games.
- Designed as a family-weight game suitable for ages 6+.
