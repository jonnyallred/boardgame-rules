## Overview

Girl Talk is a truth-or-dare party board game for 2-10 players, originally published in 1988 and popularized by Milton Bradley in the 1990s. Players spin a crystal ball spinner to receive stunts, dares, or questions. Successfully completing challenges earns points; refusing means wearing a "Zit Sticker" for the rest of the game. Every 15 points, players earn a Fortune Card from one of four categories. The first player to collect one Fortune Card from each category wins and reads their fortunes aloud.

## Components

- 1 game tray with spinner base
- 1 spinner disc (double-sided with different stunts)
- 1 crystal ball spinner knob
- 96 Fortune Cards in 4 categories (Marriage, Career, Special Moments, Children)
- Zit Sticker sheet
- Score tracker/scorekeeper
- Instructions

## Setup

1. Assemble the spinner by attaching the crystal ball knob to the spinner disc on the game tray.
2. Press out all 96 Fortune Cards and sort them face-down into 4 piles by category: Marriage, Career, Special Moments, and Children. Place them in the game tray pockets.
3. Place the Zit Sticker sheet nearby.
4. Designate a scorekeeper to track points.
5. Determine who goes first (youngest player or by agreement).

## Turn Structure

On your turn:

### 1. Spin
Spin the crystal ball knob. Read the stunt, dare, or question revealed.

### 2. Perform or Refuse
- **Perform:** Complete the stunt or answer the question within the time limit (maximum 2 minutes). If successful, earn the designated point value.
- **Refuse:** If you refuse to attempt the challenge, you must wear a Zit Sticker on your face for the rest of the game. You earn no points.

### 3. Score Points
The scorekeeper records points earned. When a player reaches 15 points, they immediately choose one Fortune Card from any of the four categories and place it face-down in front of them without reading it.

### 4. Reset Points
Points do not carry over past 15. Any excess points beyond 15 are forfeited. The player starts accumulating toward the next 15-point threshold.

### 5. Pass
Play passes to the next player clockwise.

## Actions

### Stunts and Dares
The spinner reveals various challenges such as:
- Physical stunts (act something out, do a silly dance)
- Dares (call someone, sing a song)
- Questions (share secrets, answer personal questions)
- Partner stunts (choose another player to do the challenge with you)

### Zit Stickers
Players who refuse challenges must place a Zit Sticker visibly on their face. These remain for the rest of the game as a penalty for refusing.

### Fortune Cards
Four categories provide future "fortunes":
- **Marriage:** Your future spouse
- **Career:** Your future job
- **Special Moments:** Life events
- **Children:** Number/type of children

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Points:** Earned by successfully completing spinner challenges. Point values vary by difficulty.

**Fortune Cards:** Earned at every 15-point threshold. Player chooses which category to draw from.

**Win:** The first player to collect 4 Fortune Cards (one from each category: Marriage, Career, Special Moments, Children) wins.

**Victory Celebration:** The winner reads all four Fortune Cards aloud as their "fortune." Other players return their Fortune Cards to the piles without reading them.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### Time Limit
All stunts must be completed within 2 minutes. Failure to complete within the time limit counts as a refusal.

### Refusing Challenges
Refusing is always an option, but the Zit Sticker penalty is permanent for the rest of the game.

### Double-Sided Spinner
The spinner disc is double-sided. After playing through all stunts on one side, flip the disc for new challenges.

### Partner Challenges
Some spinner results ask you to choose another player to participate with you. Both players may earn points if the challenge is cooperative.

### Multiple Games
Fortune Cards can be reshuffled for subsequent games. The double-sided spinner provides variety.
