## Overview

Captain Flip is a quick tile-drafting game where players are pirate captains recruiting crew members to fill their Adventure boards. Each turn, you draw a double-sided character tile from a bag, decide whether to keep or flip it, then place it on your board. Characters have immediate effects, end-game scoring effects, or both. The player with the most coins at the end wins — but beware the Gunners, as having 3 or more means you lose.

## Components

- 72 Character tiles (double-sided, 9 unique characters, each appearing 16 times)
- 58 Coins
- 1 large Treasure Map token
- 1 cloth bag
- 10 Adventure boards (double-sided: A, B, C, D)
- 3 player aids
- 1 rulebook

## Setup

1. Place all Character tiles in the bag.
2. Place Coins and Treasure Map in the center of the table.
3. Give each player a random Adventure board (side A for first game). Include the board with the Pirate Flag.
4. Place a Player Aid between each pair of players.
5. The player with the Pirate Flag board starts the game.

## Turn Structure

Starting with the player holding the Pirate Flag board, take turns clockwise. On your turn, follow these 4 steps:

### Step 1: Draw a Tile
Randomly draw 1 Character tile from the bag. You can only see one side.

### Step 2: Keep or Flip
Choose to **keep** this character OR **flip** the tile (turn it over). If you flip, you **must** keep the new character.

### Step 3: Place the Tile
Place the character on the **lowest empty space** of any column on your board.

### Step 4: Apply Effects
- Apply the character's **immediate effect** (yellow box), if any.
- If the tile is on the **highest space** of any column, apply the immediate bonus of that space, if any.

## Actions

### The 9 Characters

| Character | Immediate (Yellow) | End-Game (Gray) |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Cartographer** | Take the Treasure Map | — |
| **Monkey** | Take 1 coin, then flip one adjacent tile (apply new character's effects) | — |
| **Parrot** | Draw and play a new tile | Lose 1 coin |
| **Navigator** | Take 2 coins per Cartographer on your board | — |
| **Gunner** | Take 5 coins | If 3+ Gunners on your board, you lose the game |
| **Cook** | Take 1 coin per character in the Cook's row (including itself) | — |
| **Lookout** | — | 4 coins if no character above this tile in its column |
| **Carpenter** | — | 3 coins if no Gunner in its row or column |
| **Swabby** | — | 1/4/9/16/25 coins for having Swabbies in 1/2/3/4/5 columns |

### Treasure Map
If you have the Treasure Map at the end of your turn, take 1 coin. The Treasure Map can be stolen by another player who places a Cartographer.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### End of Game
When any player completes **4 columns** on their board, finish the current round (the last player is the one to the right of the Pirate Flag player). If the last player triggers the end, the game ends immediately.

### Final Scoring
1. Apply all end-game effects of your characters.
2. Score one column at a time, left to right (to avoid errors).
3. If playing board C, also apply endgame bonuses of covered spaces.

**The player with the most coins wins.** Tiebreaker: the tied player with the Treasure Map wins. Otherwise, shared victory.

**Automatic loss:** If you have 3 or more Gunners on your board, you lose regardless of coins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- You can have empty spaces in a row; columns don't need to start from the bottom row.
- Adjacency: Spaces must share a side (not diagonal) to be adjacent.
- Monkey can be placed without an adjacent tile — simply skip the flip effect.
- You can place a Cartographer even if you already have the Treasure Map (synergizes with Navigator).
- Board variants (A, B, C, D) add different space bonuses and restrictions. For first games, use side A.
- Board D spaces may require paying 1 coin to place a character there.
