## Overview

20 Questions is a party/trivia game where players take turns being the Reader (the identity to guess) while other players choose numbered clues to help them identify a well-known person, place, or thing. Points are split between the Reader and the successful Guesser based on how many clues were needed. The first player to reach the Finish space on the board wins.

## Components

- 20 Questions Game Cards (378 cards for standard version; 315 for UK; 150 for other editions)
- 4 Question Mark Playing Pieces
- 20 Red "20 Questions" Chips
- 5 Blue "Free Guess" Chips
- Game Board
- Instructions

## Setup

1. Place all playing pieces at START on the game board.
2. Place the red and blue chips along the edge of the game board.
3. Open the card deck and place a stack of cards in the center of the game board.

## Turn Structure

1. The youngest player goes first as the **Reader**. The Reader selects a card and announces: "I am a ______ (Person, Place, or Thing)" as shown at the top of the card. The Reader does not show the card to anyone.
2. The player to the Reader's **left** (the Guesser) announces a number between 1 and 20, then covers that number on the board with a red chip.
3. The Reader reads the clue matching the chosen number.
4. The Guesser has **10 seconds** to guess the Reader's identity. Players may only guess on their own turn and must choose a clue before guessing.
5. If correct: both the Guesser and Reader score (see Scoring). The player to the Reader's **right** becomes the new Reader.
6. If incorrect: play continues clockwise — the next player chooses a number, hears a clue, and guesses.
7. Continue until someone guesses correctly. There is **no penalty** for incorrect guesses.
8. At any time, players may request the Reader re-read all previously chosen clues.

## Actions

### Regular Turn
- Choose a number (1-20) not already taken
- Cover it with a red chip
- Listen to the clue
- Guess within 10 seconds (optional but recommended)

### Free Guess
- Players holding a blue Free Guess chip may play it **before** another player's turn begins
- They guess immediately before the Reader reads the next clue
- If correct, they score the same as a normal correct guess
- The Free Guess chip is returned to the board after use
- A player who uses a Free Guess before their own turn still takes their upcoming turn
- Multiple Free Guess chips may be played on a single turn

### Bonus Round
- Triggered when a player lands exactly on a Bonus Space after moving
- The Reader draws a new card and identifies it as Person, Place, or Thing
- The Bonus Player announces **5 numbers** one at a time; the Reader reads each clue
- The Bonus Player may guess **once** at any point during the 5 clues

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Each card is worth **20 points total**, divided between the Reader and the correct Guesser:

| Clues Used | Guesser Moves | Reader Moves |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 19 spaces | 1 space |
| 2 | 18 spaces | 2 spaces |
| 3 | 17 spaces | 3 spaces |
| ... | ... | ... |
| 16 | 4 spaces | 16 spaces |
| 20 | 0 spaces | 20 spaces |

**Formula:** Reader moves = number of red chips on board. Guesser moves = 20 minus red chips on board.

### Bonus Round Scoring

| Clues Used | Bonus Player Moves |
|---|---|
| 1 | 10 spaces |
| 2 | 8 spaces |
| 3 | 6 spaces |
| 4 | 4 spaces |
| 5 | 2 spaces |

The Reader does **not** score during Bonus Rounds.

### Winning
The first player to reach FINISH wins. Players need not land exactly on Finish.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Special Clue — "Take a One Free Guess Chip":** The current player guesses immediately, then takes a blue chip for future use.
- **Special Clue — "Lose your turn":** Play passes immediately to the next player; the current player may not guess.
- **Special Clue — "Go ahead" / "Go back":** Players move their pieces as directed, then guess. No player may be moved back past Start.
- **Bonus Space during mid-turn:** If a player lands on a Bonus Space during normal scoring movement, the Reader finishes the current card first (until someone guesses correctly), then the Bonus Round begins.
- **Bonus Round "Lose your turn" clue:** Ends the Bonus Round immediately.
- **Bonus Round "Go ahead"/"Go back" clue:** Player moves, then the Bonus Round continues.
- **Categories:**
  - **People:** Living or dead, real or fictional, including animals with personalities (e.g., Rin-Tin-Tin), cartoon characters, groups (e.g., The Beatles), and occupations.
  - **Places:** Geographic locations — seas, countries, rivers, cities, man-made and natural places.
  - **Things:** Non-living objects, concepts (e.g., gravity, The Equator), animals, holidays, and literature.

## Player Reference

**Turn Quick Reference:**
1. Choose a number (1-20) → cover with red chip
2. Listen to clue → guess within 10 seconds
3. Correct? Score and new Reader. Incorrect? Next player clockwise.

**Scoring Formula:**
- Guesser: 20 minus clues used = spaces moved
- Reader: clues used = spaces moved

**Bonus Scoring:** 1 clue = 10, 2 = 8, 3 = 6, 4 = 4, 5 = 2 spaces

**Card Types:** Person, Place, or Thing (announced at start of each round)
