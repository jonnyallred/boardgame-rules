## Overview

Candy Land is a simple, luck-based racing game designed for young children. Players draw colored cards and move their pieces along a winding path of colored spaces through a candy-themed landscape. No reading is required. The first player to reach "Home" wins.

## Components

- 1 game board with a colored path from Start to Home
- 4 colored playing pieces (gingerbread movers)
- 1 deck of cards (color cards and picture cards)

## Setup

1. Place the game board on the table.
2. Each player chooses a different colored playing piece.
3. Shuffle the card deck and place it face-down within reach of all players.
4. All players place their pieces at the "Start" arrow.
5. The youngest child plays first.

## Turn Structure

On your turn:
1. Draw the top card from the face-down pile.
2. Move your piece according to the card drawn.
3. Play passes to the next player.

## Actions

### Color Cards
- **Single color card:** Move forward to the **next** space matching that color.
- **Double color card:** Move forward to the **second** space matching that color.

### Picture Cards
- Move your piece to the space matching that picture (e.g., Gingerbread Cookie).
- **Important:** This may send you forward OR backward along the path.
- Proceed from the picture space on your next turn.

### Shortcuts
- **Mountain Pass:** If you land exactly on the starting space, move immediately to the other end.
- **Rainbow Trail:** If you land exactly on the starting space, move immediately to the other end.
- You only use shortcuts when you stop exactly on their starting space.

### Sticky Spaces
- **Cherry Pitfalls** and **Molasses Swamp:** Follow the directions printed on the board (typically lose a turn or wait for a specific card).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- The **first player to reach "Home"** wins the game.
- Home is reached by landing on the last blue space or drawing a card that would take you beyond it.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Two or more players may occupy the same space.
- If all cards are used before the game ends, reshuffle them and continue.
- Travel direction is always forward along the path (toward Home), except when a picture card sends you backward.
- No reading ability is required to play.
