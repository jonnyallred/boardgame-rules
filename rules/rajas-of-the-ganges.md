# Rajas of the Ganges

## Overview

A dice-placement and worker-placement game set in 16th-century India. Players are Indian nobles (Rajas/Ranis) developing their province by constructing buildings, trading goods, and navigating the Ganges River. The game features two scoring tracks (fame and money) that advance toward each other -- when they meet or cross, that player wins immediately. Dice serve as workers and currency throughout.

## Setup

1. Place main board centrally. Each player takes a province board.
2. Place market tiles, bonus tiles, and other components as directed.
3. Each player receives starting dice and places fame/money markers on their starting positions on the scoring tracks.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, place one or more workers/dice on an action space and perform that action. Then it passes to the next player.

## Actions

Players place workers (and spend dice) at four main areas of the board:

### Palace of the Maharaja
Acquire new province tiles to build in your province, gaining income or production bonuses.

### Market
Buy and sell goods using dice. Trade goods at market stalls for money or fame.

### Quarry
Gain new dice of various colors to expand your action options.

### River Ganges
Navigate your boat down the Ganges, collecting bonuses from river tiles along the way. Advance by spending dice of the appropriate values.

### Province Building
Place acquired tiles on your province board to generate ongoing benefits -- income, goods production, fame bonuses, or special abilities.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game has **two scoring tracks** running in opposite directions:
- **Fame track** (advances forward)
- **Money track** (advances forward from the other end)

**Immediate win:** The game ends immediately when a player's fame marker and money marker **meet or cross** on the tracks. That player wins.

If multiple players would cross simultaneously (unlikely), compare total advancement.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Dice values matter: higher-value dice are more powerful but harder to obtain.
- Kali tiles provide powerful one-time bonuses.
- The province board is a grid where tile placement triggers adjacency bonuses.
- Goods (silk, spices, tea, gems) are used for trading and fulfilling orders.
- The river provides a steady stream of small bonuses and is crucial for the endgame push.
