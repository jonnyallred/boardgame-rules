## Overview

Fruttirelli is a children's board game for 2-4 players published by Zoch Verlag. Players help parent animals (hedgehog, hare, squirrel, and badger) forage for food and bring provisions back to their families. The game features collectible food items including apples, mushrooms, and berries scattered across the board. The player who returns to their family first while bringing sufficient provisions for everyone wins the game.

## Components

- Game board (depicting a forest/countryside with paths)
- 4 animal figures (hedgehog, hare, squirrel, badger)
- Food tokens (apples, mushrooms, berries)
- Die or spinner
- Family/home tiles

## Setup

1. Place the game board in the center of the table.
2. Each player chooses an animal and places it on the starting position.
3. Distribute food tokens across the board on designated spaces.
4. Place family/home tiles at each player's home location.
5. Youngest player goes first.

## Turn Structure

On your turn:

### 1. Roll the Die
Roll the die to determine how many spaces to move or which action to take.

### 2. Move Your Animal
Move your parent animal along the path the number of spaces shown on the die.

### 3. Collect Food
If you land on a space with food (apples, mushrooms, or berries), you may collect the food token and add it to your provisions.

### 4. Pass
Play passes to the next player clockwise.

## Actions

### Foraging
Land on food spaces to collect provisions. Different food types may have different values or requirements.

### Returning Home
Once you have collected enough provisions for your family, head back to your home/family tile. You must have sufficient food for all family members.

### Path Choices
The board may offer multiple paths, allowing players to choose routes that maximize food collection opportunities.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Win:** The first player to return to their family with sufficient provisions for everyone wins.

**Sufficient Provisions:** You must collect enough food items (the required number varies based on family size) before returning home.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### Food Requirements
Each animal family requires a specific number of food items. Returning home without enough food is not allowed (or does not count as winning).

### Shared Spaces
Multiple animals may occupy the same space. Food tokens on a space are taken by the first animal to arrive.

### Die Results
Some die results may trigger special events (wild animals, shortcuts, or detours).
