## Overview

Pony Express is a racing and bluffing game set in the Old West. Players are wild riders of the Pony Express, racing from St. Joseph, Missouri to Sacramento, California. The game combines poker-style dice rolling with a race across the frontier, featuring bluffing, gunfights, saloons, gold mines, and Indian encounters. The first rider to deliver mail to Sacramento wins.

## Components

- 1 game board (trail from St. Joseph to Sacramento, 19 spaces per trail)
- 5 rider cards (one per player color)
- 5 rider discs
- 5 dice (numbered A-B-C-D-1-0)
- 3 Indian meeples
- Gold tokens
- Item cards
- Dice shaker

## Setup

1. Place the game board on the table.
2. Each player chooses a rider and takes their rider disc.
3. Place riders at St. Joseph (start).
4. Shuffle item cards and deal one to each player.
5. Place the 3 Indian meeples on the 3 red areas near Sacramento.
6. Place gold tokens near the board as a bank.

## Turn Structure

### 1. Receive Charity
If the active player starts their turn with no gold, they receive 1 gold from the bank.

### 2. Roll the Dice
The active player rolls five dice in the shaker and places them face-down on the table. They then discreetly lift to look at the rolled results before showing them to other players.

### 3. Spend Gold to Reroll (Optional)
The active player may pay 1 gold per reroll attempt. They may remove some dice, reroll them, and keep others. Multiple rerolls are possible as long as gold is spent. At least 1 die must be kept from each roll.

### 4. Stake Your Claim
The active player announces a poker hand claim (what they allegedly rolled). The claimed hand determines movement spaces.

| Claimed Hand | Movement |
|-------------|----------|
| Pair | 1 space |
| Two Pairs | 2 spaces |
| Three of a Kind | 2 spaces |
| Straight | 3 spaces |
| Full House | 3 spaces |
| Four of a Kind | 4 spaces |
| Five of a Kind | 5 spaces |

### 5. Call the Bluff
Other players may call the bluff. If called:
- **If the claim was true:** The caller loses; the active player moves as claimed.
- **If the claim was a bluff:** The bluffing player is sent to prison.

### 6. Reveal the Dice and Move
If not accused of bluffing, or if the bluff was genuine, move the rider forward the claimed number of spaces.

## Actions

### Action Spaces
When a rider lands on an action space:
- **Railway Station:** Move to the indicated railway station further ahead.
- **Indians:** Roll dice to interact; may gain or lose movement.
- **Gold Mine:** Roll dice to gain gold.
- **Saloon:** Other riders may drink; pay gold for effects.
- **Relay Station:** Draw item cards.

### Gunfight or Poker Game
When multiple riders occupy the same space, a gunfight or poker game may occur.

### Item Cards
Each player may hold item cards with various effects. Items must be played at the right time.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Two Ways to Win
1. **"Jackrabbit" victory:** The first rider to reach Sacramento wins immediately.
2. **"Last man standing":** If a second rider reaches Sacramento before the first rider's third mailbox, there is a final duel. The winner of the duel wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Prison: A rider sent to prison spends their next turn in prison, then rolls dice to leave.
- Riders on the same space may trigger duels.
- Gold is used for rerolling dice and various action space effects.
- Indian meeples interact with riders passing through the red areas near Sacramento.
- The trail is 19 spaces long; the town of Sacramento counts as the last space.
