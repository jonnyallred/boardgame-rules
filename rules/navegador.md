# Navegador

## Overview

Navegador is a strategy game about the Portuguese Age of Discoveries. Players represent wealthy trade dynasties building the Portuguese colonial empire by exploring sea regions, founding colonies, building factories/shipyards/churches, and acquiring privileges. Actions are selected via a rondel mechanism -- on each turn, a player moves their marker along a circular track to choose their action. The player with the most victory points after the final round wins.

## Components

- 1 Game board with rondel, sea regions, market, buildings, and privilege gallery
- 36 Ships, 5 Workers, 5 Game stones, 12 Wooden disks (explorers)
- 10 Churches, 10 Shipyards, 23 Factories, 3 Price markers
- 5 Player sheets, 33 Colony tokens, 35 Privilege tokens
- 1 Navegador card, 2 Double loss markers
- Coins (10, 50, 100, 200 Cruzados)

## Setup

1. Place price markers in the 4th row of the market for sugar, gold, and spices.
2. Each player starts with 2 ships in Portugal, 1 worker in Lisboa (column 3), game stone in middle of rondel, 200 Cruzados, a King's privilege, 5 remaining ships in supply, and a player sheet with 1 factory, 1 shipyard, and 1 church.
3. Place colony token stacks, privileges in the gallery arches, explorer disks on sea regions, and buildings in the lower-right area.

## Turn Structure

Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, move your game stone forward on the rondel to select an action. The first 3 spaces ahead are free; each additional space costs 1 ship (returned to supply). You cannot stay on the same space twice in a row.

## Actions

There are 7 actions on the rondel (Market appears twice):

### Workers
Recruit new workers: 50 Cruzados each (up to 1 per church owned). Additional workers cost 100 Cruzados per game phase. Maximum 9 workers.

### Ships
Build new ships using the same pricing as workers: 50 Cruzados each (up to 1 per shipyard owned). Additional ships cost extra per phase.

### Sailing
Move your ships forward through sea regions. Ships must move as a fleet from one region to the next unexplored region or any previously explored region.

### Exploring
When your ships enter an unexplored sea region: claim the explorer disk (worth VP), receive a cash bonus, but lose 1 ship. Exploring certain regions triggers phase changes (Phase II after Cabo da Boa Esperanca, Phase III after Malaca).

### Colony
Found colonies by spending workers: place a worker on an available colony token in a region where you have ships. Take the colony token (shows goods type). Colonies produce goods for market selling.

### Market (Sell/Buy)
- **Sell:** Sell goods from your colonies. Each colony token of a type earns the current market price. Market price drops as goods are sold.
- **Buy (Process):** Factories process goods. Each factory earns the current market price for its good type. Market price rises as goods are bought.

### Privilege
Spend 1 worker to acquire a privilege token from the gallery. Privileges multiply VP in 5 categories: colonies, factories, explorers, shipyards, churches.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends after the last sea region is explored or the last building is taken; one final round is played.

VP scored from 5 categories, each multiplied by privileges:
- Colonies, Factories, Explorers, Shipyards, Churches
- Base VP shown on player sheet; privileges increase multiplier
- Additional 1 VP per: worker, ship, full 200 Cruzados

**Highest total VP wins.**

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Navegador card:** Held by the last player initially. Allows combining any turn with an additional sailing action. Passed right after use. Cannot be used in the very first round.
- **Phase triggers:** Red border sea regions trigger phase changes. Phases affect worker/ship costs.
- **Double ship loss:** Certain distant regions (Nagasaki, Macau with 2-3 players) cause loss of 2 ships when exploring.
- **Market mechanism:** Selling colonies pushes prices down; buying with factories pushes prices up. The market creates dynamic tension between colony and factory strategies.
