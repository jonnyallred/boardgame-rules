## Overview

Bull's-Eye Ball is an electronic dexterity game where players bounce small metal balls off a trampoline into scoring zones on a rotating game unit. The game features multiple play modes including timed challenges and point targets. Players aim to score points by landing balls in the bull's-eye, inner ring, and outer ring of the spinning target.

## Components

- 1 electronic game unit with spinning target, trampoline, and light/sound effects
- 10 metal balls
- Battery compartment (requires batteries, not included)

## Setup

1. Insert batteries into the game unit.
2. Turn the game on using the "on/off" button or "turbo on" switch.
3. Place the 10 metal balls in the designated drop-in tray.
4. Select a game mode.

## Turn Structure

Game play varies by mode. The basic cycle is:

1. Select a game mode by pressing the appropriate button.
2. When the game signals to start, begin bouncing balls off the trampoline.
3. Aim for the spinning target — the bull's-eye scores the most points.
4. The game announces scores via electronic sound effects and lights.
5. The mode ends when time runs out or the point target is reached.

## Actions

### Bouncing Technique
- Drop or toss a metal ball onto the trampoline surface.
- The ball bounces upward toward the rotating target.
- Aim and angle your tosses to land balls in the scoring zones.

### Scoring Zones
| Zone | Points |
|------|--------|
| Bull's-eye (center) | 3 points |
| Inner ring | 2 points |
| Outer ring | 1 point |

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Game Modes
The game includes 5 exciting modes:

1. **30 Second Blitz:** Score as many points as possible in 30 seconds.
2. **25 Point Rush:** Be the first to reach exactly 25 points.
3. **Bulls-Eye Ace:** Score a set number of bull's-eyes to win.
4. **Target Master:** Hit specific targets as called out by the game.
5. **Practice Mode:** Free play with no time limit or point target.

In multiplayer modes, players take turns or play simultaneously depending on the mode selected.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- If the game unit does not respond, press the RESET button on the bottom of the unit.
- Balls that miss the target entirely score 0 points.
- The spinning target changes speed in some modes, increasing difficulty.
- Keep the game on a flat, stable surface for best results.
- If hands get tired during extended play, take a break.
