# Mine a Million

## Overview

Mine a Million (also known as The Business Game) is a 1965 economic board game by Waddingtons. Players manage a mining business, producing units of tin and transporting them to ports for sale. The game progresses through three phases: using lorries, establishing canal barges, and finally shipping goods by sea. The first player to earn one million dollars wins.

## Components

- 1 game board (mining area, canals, and sea routes to 3 ports)
- Player tokens and money
- Vehicle tokens (lorries, barges, ships)
- Production units
- Dice

## Setup

1. Place the board centrally.
2. Each player takes starting money and positions their mining operation.
3. Place vehicle tokens in their starting locations.
4. Determine the starting player.

## Turn Structure

On your turn:
1. **Roll dice** and move your transport vehicle.
2. **Produce** units at your mine.
3. **Transport** units via available vehicles (lorry, barge, or ship).
4. **Sell** units at ports for money.
5. **Invest** in better transport infrastructure.

## Actions

### Production
- Mines produce units each turn.
- Units must be loaded onto transport vehicles.

### Transport
- **Phase 1 (Lorries):** Transport units by road. Limited capacity.
- **Phase 2 (Canals):** Establish canal companies. Barges carry more but are restricted to canal routes.
- **Phase 3 (Sea):** Ship goods across the sea to distant ports for higher prices.

### Selling
- Three ports offer different prices:
  - Nearby ports: lower prices
  - Port Boston (farthest): highest prices
- Selling at Port Boston is the most profitable.

### Vehicles
- Vehicles cannot be purchased outright; they are leased.
- To lease, a player must have enough units to fill the vehicle (or contract from other players).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The first player to accumulate **one million dollars** wins.

The fastest path: get 10 units to Port Boston (farthest) and 5 units to either of the nearer ports.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Vehicles require minimum cargo to lease.
- Players can contract cargo space from other players.
- The three-phase progression represents technological advancement.
- The game is a classic of British board gaming.

## Player Reference

**Turn:** Roll -> Produce -> Transport -> Sell -> Invest

**Transport phases:** Lorries (early) -> Canal barges (mid) -> Ships (late)

**Win condition:** First to $1,000,000
