## Overview

Hitler's Last Gamble is an operational-level wargame published by 3W in 1989 that simulates the Battle of the Bulge — the last major German offensive on the Western Front in December 1944. The game is played on two paper maps covering the Ardennes region. One player commands the German forces attempting a breakthrough, while the other controls the Allied defenders. Praised by the Chicago Tribune as potentially "the best Bulge game since SPI's old Wacht am Rhein," it received 4 out of 5 stars from Games International magazine.

## Components

- 2 Paper maps (Ardennes region)
- Unit counters (German and Allied forces)
- Combat Results Table (CRT)
- Turn track
- Charts and tables
- Die/dice
- Rules booklet

## Setup

1. Lay out both paper maps to form the complete Ardennes battlefield.
2. Place German units at their historical starting positions for December 1944.
3. Place Allied units at their defensive positions.
4. Set the turn track to turn 1.
5. Organize charts and CRT for reference.

## Turn Structure

Each game turn represents a period of the battle and consists of:
1. **German Phase** — German player moves and attacks.
2. **Allied Phase** — Allied player moves and attacks.
3. **Supply Phase** — Both sides check supply status.
4. **Administrative Phase** — Advance turn marker, check victory conditions.

## Actions

### Movement
- Units move using movement points, spending different amounts per terrain type.
- Terrain includes forests, roads, rivers, towns, and the Ardennes hills.

### Combat
- Attacks are resolved using odds-based Combat Results Table.
- Compare attacking strength to defending strength for a ratio.
- Roll dice and consult the CRT for results (attacker/defender losses, retreats, breakthroughs).

### Supply
- Units must trace supply lines to supply sources.
- Unsupplied units suffer combat and movement penalties.
- German supply is limited, reflecting historical logistics challenges.

### Reinforcements
- Both sides receive reinforcements on specific turns.
- Allied reinforcements increase as the battle progresses.
- German reinforcements reflect the limited reserves available.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- The German player wins by achieving specific territorial objectives (capturing key towns, crossroads, and supply depots) within the time limit.
- The Allied player wins by preventing German objectives or by destroying sufficient German forces.
- The game may use victory point scoring based on territory controlled, losses inflicted, and objectives achieved.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Two-map scale:** The large playing area provides detailed simulation of the Ardennes terrain.
- **Weather effects:** Weather conditions affect movement, combat, and air support availability.
- **German fuel shortage:** German forces face historical fuel supply limitations that constrain offensive operations.
- **Allied air power:** Allied air support becomes increasingly effective as weather clears, shifting the balance.
- **Bridge demolition:** Allied player may attempt to destroy bridges to slow the German advance.
- **Surprise factor:** Early turns may include rules for the initial German surprise attack.
