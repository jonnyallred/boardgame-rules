## Overview

Asteroid Pirates: A Game of Ship to Ship Combat in the Asteroid Belts is a tactical space combat board game published by Yaquinto Publications in 1980. Players command spacecraft engaged in combat within asteroid belts. The game features ship-to-ship combat with energy management, weapons systems, and maneuvering through asteroid fields. Players must balance power allocation between weapons, shields, and movement while navigating hazardous terrain.

## Components

- Game board or hex map (depicting asteroid belt)
- Ship counters/tokens
- Energy allocation sheets
- Dice
- Asteroid counters
- Rules booklet

## Setup

1. Place the game map showing the asteroid belt.
2. Each player selects their ships and places them in starting positions.
3. Prepare energy allocation sheets for each ship.
4. Distribute asteroid counters on the map as specified by the scenario.

## Turn Structure

Each turn follows a sequence:
1. **Energy Allocation:** Players secretly allocate energy to weapons, shields, and movement.
2. **Movement:** Ships move according to their allocated energy.
3. **Combat:** Ships within range fire weapons at targets.
4. **Damage Resolution:** Apply damage to hit ships.
5. **Asteroid Hazards:** Check for asteroid collisions.

## Actions

- **Allocate energy:** Distribute power between ship systems (weapons, shields, movement).
- **Move ship:** Navigate through the asteroid belt.
- **Fire weapons:** Attack enemy ships within weapon range.
- **Raise shields:** Protect against incoming fire.
- **Evade:** Use movement to avoid attacks and asteroids.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Victory conditions depend on the scenario:
- Destroy all enemy ships
- Control a strategic location
- Survive for a set number of turns
- Escort a ship safely through the asteroid belt

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Asteroid collisions:** Ships that enter asteroid hexes may take damage.
- **Energy management:** The core mechanic; allocating too much to one system leaves others vulnerable.
- **Hidden allocation:** Energy allocation is done secretly, adding a bluffing element.
- **Ship variety:** Different ship classes have different energy capacities and weapon loadouts.
