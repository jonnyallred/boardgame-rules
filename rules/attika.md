## Overview

Attika is a tile-placement strategy game set in ancient Greece. Each player leads a Greek city-state, constructing buildings across a shared landscape. Players win by either being the first to place all 30 of their buildings on the board or by connecting any two shrines with a chain of their buildings. The game combines hand management, resource management, and spatial planning.

## Components

- 22 game board tiles (hexagonal landscape tiles)
- 4 shrines (assembled from 2 parts each)
- 5 amphorae tokens
- 4 player storage boards
- 120 building tiles (30 per player, in 4 different colours)
- 60 landscape cards

## Setup

1. Place 4-8 game board tiles face-up (depending on player count), along with 2-4 shrines positioned on the board.
2. Each player takes their 30 building tiles.
3. Sort tiles into stacks: 1 stack of 6 main buildings and 3 stacks of 8 other buildings. Each stack is placed face-down.
4. Draw the top tile from each stack and place them face-up on your player board.
5. The starting player draws 4 landscape cards. The second player draws 5, the third draws 6, and the fourth draws 7.

## Turn Structure

On each turn, a player performs **one** of two options:

### Option A: Draw 2 New Buildings
- Draw 2 building tiles from the tops of your face-down stacks.
- For each tile drawn, you may either:
  - Pay the building cost to place it immediately on the game board, OR
  - Keep it on your player board for later.

### Option B: Build 3 Buildings
- Select up to 3 building tiles from your player board.
- Pay the construction cost for each and place them on the game board.

## Actions

### Placement Rules
- A building may be placed on **any empty space** on the game board, regardless of whether the space has a landscape symbol.
- Buildings of the same player must form connected groups where possible.

### Construction Costs
- Each building tile shows a cost in landscape symbols.
- The cost is **reduced** by landscape symbols on the building's destination space plus adjacent empty spaces.
- Remaining costs are paid using landscape cards from your hand.
- **Any two landscape cards** may substitute for one required card of a different type.

### Free Building (Arrow Sequence)
- Buildings can be constructed for **free** by following the arrow sequence on the player board.
- The prerequisite building (the one the arrow points from) must already exist on the game board.
- The new building must be placed **adjacent** to the prerequisite building.

### Streets
- Streets cost 5 landscape cards (reduced by applicable landscape symbols).
- Streets are built free when placed adjacent to an existing street.

### Settlements
- New settlements (disconnected groups) incur an additional cost of **1 landscape card per existing settlement** you have on the board.
- Buildings connected through shrines do **not** form a single settlement.

### Amphorae
- When all buildings of a group are connected on the game board, the player earns **1 amphora**.
- An amphora can be spent to either:
  - Add 1 extra draw or build action to your turn, OR
  - Draw 1 landscape card.
- Multiple amphorae may be used in a single turn.

### Board Expansion
- When a player draws the last building tile from one of their 4 stacks:
  1. Place that tile on the board (paying the building cost).
  2. Draw a new game board tile from the stack and add it to the board, connected to existing tiles or shrines.

### Forfeiting Actions for Cards
- Instead of drawing buildings or building, a player may forfeit draw/build actions to draw landscape cards.
- 1 card per forfeited action, or 3 cards if forfeiting all actions for the turn.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends immediately when either victory condition is met:

1. **Construction Victory:** A player places all **30** of their buildings on the game board.
2. **Connection Victory:** A player creates an unbroken chain of their buildings connecting **any two shrines**.

The player achieving either condition wins immediately.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Shrine connections:** Shrines do not unify settlements. A chain through a shrine for victory purposes must consist entirely of the player's buildings.
- **Two-card substitution:** When paying construction costs, any 2 landscape cards of any type can substitute for 1 required card.
- **Board tiles and shrines:** New board tiles must connect to existing tiles or shrines when placed.
- **Settlement cost:** The extra cost for new settlements makes it advantageous to keep buildings connected.
- **Group completion bonus:** Plan to complete building groups for amphorae bonuses.

## Player Reference

**Turn Options:**
- A) Draw 2 buildings (may place immediately or keep)
- B) Build up to 3 buildings from player board

**Victory Conditions:**
- Place all 30 buildings, OR
- Connect any 2 shrines with your buildings

**Free Building:** Follow arrow sequence on player board; place adjacent to prerequisite

**Amphorae Uses:** +1 action OR draw 1 landscape card

**Settlement Penalty:** +1 landscape card per existing settlement when starting a new one

**Card Substitution:** Any 2 cards = 1 required card
