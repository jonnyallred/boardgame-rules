## Overview

Candamir: The First Settlers is an adventure game set on the island of Catan. Players are settlers who must explore the wilderness, collect resources and ingredients, complete adventures, and deliver goods to the village founders (Candamir, Osmund, and Jared) in exchange for victory points. Each character has unique abilities that improve through experience. The first player to earn 10 victory points wins.

## Components

- 1 game board (with village center and exploration areas)
- 48 wooden pieces (4 figures, 40 VP cubes, 4 round disks)
- 48 hexagonal Exploration tiles (sorted by color)
- 4 Character boards
- 12 Potion tiles
- 8 Equipment markers
- 4 Endurance markers
- 16 Experience markers
- 90 Resource and Ingredient cards
- 29 Movement cards
- 22 Adventure cards (sorted into decks 1, 2, 3)
- 8 Character cards (4 male, 4 female)
- 1 wooden die
- 2 card holders
- 1 Shuffle Cards board

## Setup

1. Place the board in the center. Place exploration tiles face-down (number side up) on matching color and number spaces. Remove tiles based on player count: 2 players remove "3" and "4" tiles; 3 players remove "4" tiles; 4 players use all.
2. Each player takes a color: 10 VP markers, 1 destination token, 1 adventurer figure. Place adventurers in the village.
3. Deal 1 female character card to each player (may swap for male equivalent). Each character has unique starting stats.
4. Sort resources (ore, hide, lumber) and ingredients (herbs, honey, mushrooms) into card holders. Each player starts with 1 lumber card.
5. Sort adventure cards by number (1, 2, 3). Stack: 3 on bottom, 2 in middle, 1 on top. Reveal top 3 face-up.
6. Prepare potions, equipment, endurance, and experience markers near the board.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, perform these steps in order:

### 1. Set a Destination
Place your destination marker on any exploration tile on the board. This is where you are heading.

### 2. Travel
Draw movement cards one at a time. Each card may show:
- **Safe passage:** Continue traveling.
- **Encounter:** Must resolve an encounter (using your character's abilities).
- **Arrival:** You reach your destination.

The number of movement cards you draw depends on the distance to your destination.

### 3. Encounter Resolution
When you encounter wildlife, bandits, or other challenges, use your character stats (Strength, Agility, Charisma, or Stamina) to resolve them. Roll the die and add your relevant ability score. Meet or exceed the target to succeed.

**Success:** Gain rewards (resources, ingredients, experience).
**Failure:** Suffer consequences (lose resources, return to village).

### 4. Destination Actions
When you arrive at your destination tile, flip it face-up (if not already revealed) and gain the listed benefit (resources, ingredients, or special effects).

### 5. Village Actions (if in village)
When in the village, you may:
- **Trade with founders:** Deliver specific combinations of resources/ingredients to Candamir, Osmund, or Jared in exchange for VP.
- **Complete adventures:** Fulfill adventure card requirements for VP.
- **Buy equipment:** Spend resources to gain equipment that boosts abilities.
- **Brew potions:** Combine ingredients to create potions with various effects.

## Actions

### Character Abilities
Each character has 4 abilities: Strength, Agility, Charisma, Stamina. Each starts at different values. Gain experience markers to improve abilities during the game.

### Equipment
Equipment markers boost specific abilities. Purchased with resources in the village.

### Potions
Brewed from ingredient combinations. Provide temporary or permanent bonuses during exploration.

### Adventures
The 3 face-up adventure cards show specific delivery requirements. Fulfilling one earns VP and reveals a new adventure.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

VP are earned by:
- Delivering goods to village founders
- Completing adventure cards
- Certain exploration tile bonuses

**The first player to reach 10 VP wins immediately.**

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Each character has a unique special ability that provides a specific advantage.
- Failed encounters may force you back to the village.
- You cannot look at face-down exploration tiles; they are revealed when visited.
- Resources and ingredients in hand are kept secret from other players.
- Potion effects vary by type and can significantly affect encounters.
- Adventure cards increase in difficulty as the game progresses (deck 1 is easiest, deck 3 hardest).
