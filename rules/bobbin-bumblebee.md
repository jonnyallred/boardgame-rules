## Overview

Bobbin' Bumblebee is a children's dexterity game for 2 to 4 players, ages 3 and up. A mechanical bumblebee flies around the hive, and players must bop it with their wand at the right moment to protect their honeycomb discs. When the bee is flipped over toward an opponent, it "steals" their honey. The last player with at least one honeycomb disc remaining wins.

## Components

- 1 mechanical hive base unit with spinning bee mechanism
- 1 bobbin' bumblebee figure
- 4 player wands
- 12 honeycomb discs (3 per player)

## Setup

1. Place the hive base in the center of the table.
2. Each player takes a wand and 3 honeycomb discs, placing the discs in front of them.
3. Players sit evenly spaced around the hive.
4. Activate the mechanical bee.

## Turn Structure

The game is played in real time — there are no formal turns. The mechanical bee spins continuously around the hive, and players react by bopping it at the right moment.

## Actions

**Bop the Bee:**
- When the bee approaches your position, use your wand to bop (hit) it.
- Successfully bopping the bee flips it over so it strikes toward the opponent to your immediate left.
- The bee rotates around the hive in a counter-clockwise direction.

**Lose a Honeycomb:**
- If the bee reaches your position and you fail to bop it (or miss), you lose one honeycomb disc.
- Remove one disc from your supply.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The last player with at least one honeycomb disc remaining wins the game.

A player is eliminated when they lose all 3 of their honeycomb discs.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Real-Time Play:** There are no turns — the game is continuous and reactive. Players must stay alert at all times.
- **Young Children:** Designed for ages 3+, the game develops hand-eye coordination and reflexes in young players.
- **Bee Direction:** The bee moves counter-clockwise, so bopping it sends it toward the player on your left.
- **Quick Game:** With only 3 discs per player, games are very short (10-15 minutes), making it ideal for young attention spans.
