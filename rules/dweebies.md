## Overview

Dweebies is a card-matching game in which players place Dweebie cards on a shared grid and collect entire lines of cards by matching the Dweebie at both ends of a row or column. The player who collects the most cards wins.

## Components

- 54 cards featuring 27 different Dweebies (each Dweebie appears 1-3 times in the deck, indicated by dots on the card)

## Setup

1. Shuffle the deck and deal each player 5 face-down cards. Look at your cards but keep them secret.
2. Stack the remaining cards in a face-down draw pile within reach of all players.

## Turn Structure

The person with the least amount of hair goes first. Play continues to the left. On your turn, perform these 3 steps in order:

1. **Place a card** from your hand face up on the playing surface.
2. **Collect Dweebies** if you made a match.
3. **Draw a card** from the draw pile.

## Actions

### Placing Cards
- If no cards are in play, place your card in the center.
- Otherwise, place your card adjacent (above, below, left, or right) to an existing card. Diagonal placement is not allowed.

### Collecting Dweebies
- Cards form lines going horizontally and vertically.
- If the Dweebie you placed at one end of a line matches the Dweebie at the **other end** of the same line, you collect **all cards in that line** (including both end cards). Place them face down in front of you.
- Matching Dweebies must be at the very ends of a line, not in the middle.
- You may **not** collect diagonal lines.

### Keep Dweebies Together
- If collecting a line splits the remaining cards into disconnected groups, subsequent players must place cards to **reconnect** the groups by the shortest possible link before anyone may collect Dweebies again.

### Drawing Cards
- Draw 1 card from the draw pile at the end of your turn.
- If you forget to draw, you may draw at any later time.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

When the draw pile runs out, players continue playing cards from their hands until everyone is out of cards. The player who has collected the **most cards** wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- The number of dots under the Dweebie image on a card indicates how many copies of that Dweebie exist in the deck (1, 2, or 3).
- Cards must always be placed orthogonally adjacent to existing cards; never diagonally.
- No collecting is allowed while the Dweebie grid is split into disconnected groups. Players must bridge gaps first.
- If you forget to draw at end of turn, you may draw at any other time.

## Player Reference

**Turn Sequence:**
1. Place 1 card (adjacent to existing cards)
2. Collect matching line (if ends match)
3. Draw 1 card

**Collecting Rule:** Match the Dweebie at both ends of a horizontal or vertical line to collect the entire line.

**Reconnection Rule:** If collecting splits the grid, bridge the gap before collecting again.

**End Game:** Draw pile empty + all hands empty = game over. Most cards wins.
