## Overview

Assault Across the Suez is a tactical-level wargame covering the Turkish 25th and 10th divisions' epic bid to capture and destroy a portion of the Suez Canal in February 1915 during World War I. The game covers platoon and company-level combat, individual ships, and the British defense of the canal. One player controls the attacking Ottoman Turkish forces, while the other defends with British and Allied troops.

## Components

- Game map (depicting the Suez Canal area)
- Counters representing military units (platoons, companies, ships)
- Combat Results Table
- Terrain Effects Chart
- Dice
- Rules booklet

## Setup

1. Place the game map showing the Suez Canal zone.
2. Each player takes their assigned counters (Ottoman/British).
3. Deploy units according to historical setup instructions.
4. Place the turn marker on the first turn.

## Turn Structure

Standard wargame alternating turn sequence:
1. **Movement Phase:** Active player moves units.
2. **Combat Phase:** Resolve attacks against adjacent enemy units.
3. **Second player Movement Phase.**
4. **Second player Combat Phase.**
5. **End Phase:** Check victory conditions, advance turn.

## Actions

- **Move units:** Advance platoons and companies across the desert toward or away from the canal.
- **Attack:** Engage enemy forces in combat using the Combat Results Table.
- **Naval support:** Ships in the canal can provide fire support for the defending British forces.
- **Cross the canal:** Turkish forces must attempt canal crossings under fire.
- **Defend positions:** British forces dig in and defend the canal banks.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- **Turkish Victory:** Successfully cross the Suez Canal and establish a bridgehead, or destroy enough canal infrastructure.
- **British Victory:** Repel the Turkish assault and prevent canal crossings.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Canal crossing:** Special rules govern attempting to cross the Suez Canal under fire, one of the most difficult tactical challenges in the game.
- **Ship combat:** Individual ships have separate combat and movement rules.
- **Desert terrain:** Movement through desert is costly and affects supply.
- **WWI setting:** Unlike many wargames set in WWII, this covers a WWI desert campaign.

## Player Reference

**Scale:** Platoon/company level

**Sides:** Ottoman Turkish (attacker) vs. British/Allied (defender)

**Key challenge:** Canal crossing under fire
