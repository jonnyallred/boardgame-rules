# Quixo

## Overview

Quixo is an abstract strategy game for 2 or 4 players (in teams). On a 5x5 board of cubes, players take turns removing a cube from the periphery, changing its face to their symbol, and sliding it back in -- pushing an entire row or column. The goal is to form a line of 5 cubes bearing your symbol.

## Components

| Component | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Game board (5x5 grid) | 1 |
| Cubes (blank on top; circle on one side, cross on another) | 25 |

## Setup

1. Place all 25 cubes in the board with blank faces up.
2. Choose symbols: one player/team plays crosses, the other circles.
3. Determine who starts.

## Turn Structure

On your turn (you may never skip):
1. **Choose a cube** from the board's periphery (outer ring of 16 positions). You may take a blank cube or one showing your own symbol. You may **never** take a cube showing your opponent's symbol.
2. **Change the face** to show your symbol on top.
3. **Replace the cube** at one end of the incomplete row/column created when the cube was removed. Push the remaining cubes to fill the gap. You may **never** replace the cube in the position from which it was taken.

## Actions

The key action is selecting which cube to take and where to re-insert it. Re-insertion pushes an entire row or column, potentially disrupting existing lines or creating new ones.

In the first round, all cubes are blank, so players must take blank cubes.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Win:** Be the first to make and announce a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line of 5 cubes bearing your symbol.

**Lose:** If you make a line of 5 cubes bearing your **opponent's** symbol, you lose -- even if you simultaneously make a line of your own symbol.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- You cannot skip your turn.
- Only cubes on the periphery (outer ring) can be taken.
- The center 9 cubes (3x3 inner grid) can never be directly taken, only pushed.
- A game typically lasts 10-20 minutes.

### 4-Player Rules (Teams)
- Teams of 2, partners sitting opposite each other.
- Play clockwise; teams alternate turns (A1, B1, A2, B2).
- Each player may only take a cube with their symbol pointing toward them (directional arrows determine ownership within a team).
- Teams may agree before the game whether conferring is permitted.
- A team loses if they form a line of the opposing team's symbol.

## Player Reference

| Step | Rule |
|---|---|
| Take cube | Periphery only; blank or your symbol |
| Change face | Must show your symbol |
| Replace cube | Push into any end of the row/column; not the original position |
| Win | Line of 5 of your symbol |
| Lose | Form a line of 5 of opponent's symbol |
