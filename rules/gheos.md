## Overview

Gheos is a tile-laying civilization game for 2-4 players in which players act as gods shaping the world by placing triangular tiles depicting land and water. As continents form, civilizations arise, expand, and sometimes go to war. Players collect followers of different colored civilizations and use three scoring tokens at strategic moments to score points based on their followers multiplied by the civilizations' achievements. Nine Epoch tiles mixed into the draw pile punctuate the game with automatic scoring. When the eighth Epoch tile is drawn, the game ends with a final scoring round.

## Components

- Triangular tiles (depicting combinations of land and water, with wheat, sword, and cup symbols)
- 9 Epoch tiles (mixed into the draw pile)
- Colored discs (civilization markers placed on continents)
- Colored cubes (follower tokens for each player)
- 3 scoring tokens per player
- Score track
- Starting tile

## Setup

1. Place the starting tile in the center of the play area.
2. Each player takes 3 scoring tokens and their color of follower cubes.
3. Each player draws 2 tiles to form their starting hand.
4. Shuffle all remaining tiles (including 9 Epoch tiles) face-down to form the draw pile.
5. Determine starting player randomly.

## Turn Structure

On your turn:

### 1. Place a Tile
Place one tile from your hand adjacent to any existing tile on the board. Tiles are triangular and create land masses (continents) and sea areas.

### 2. Resolve Tile Effects
Depending on what the placed tile creates:
- **New continent created:** Place a colored disc on it to represent a new civilization. Gain follower cubes of that color equal to the number of wheat symbols on the new continent.
- **Continent expanded (no new continent):** You may take 1 follower cube of any civilization currently on the board.
- **Continents merged:** If tile placement connects two separate continents, their civilizations may merge or go to war (based on sword symbols).
- **Continent split:** If tile placement divides a continent, civilizations may split.

### 3. Optional: Score (spend scoring token)
At any point during your turn, you may spend one of your 3 scoring tokens to score one civilization. You earn points equal to your followers of that color multiplied by the number of cup symbols in territories that civilization controls.

### 4. Draw a Tile
Draw a tile from the face-down draw pile. If an Epoch tile is drawn, resolve it immediately (see Epoch Scoring), then draw another tile.

## Actions

### Tile Placement
Unlike most tile-laying games, Gheos is not edge-matching. Tiles can go in any valid position adjacent to existing tiles.

### Civilization Expansion
When a continent grows without creating a new one, players choose to take a follower of any existing civilization. Building up a large stock of followers in a strong civilization is key to high-scoring opportunities.

### War
When two continents with different civilizations merge through tile placement, war may occur. The civilization with more sword symbols in its territory survives; the other is destroyed. Players with followers of the destroyed civilization lose those followers.

### Scoring Tokens
Each player has exactly 3 scoring tokens for the entire game. When spent, the player scores: (number of their followers in chosen civilization) x (number of cup symbols in that civilization's territory). Timing is critical -- score too early and miss growth; score too late and risk the civilization being destroyed.

### Epoch Scoring
When an Epoch tile is drawn from the draw pile, every player scores 1 point per pyramid symbol on the board. The Epoch tile is set aside and a new tile is drawn. When the 8th Epoch tile is drawn, the game ends immediately.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**During the Game:**
- Scoring tokens: followers x cups for chosen civilization
- Epoch tiles: 1 point per pyramid for all players

**Game End (8th Epoch tile):**
All remaining civilizations score one final time. Each player scores points for each civilization based on followers held multiplied by cup symbols.

**Winner:** The player with the most total points wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### Civilization Destruction
When war eliminates a civilization, all followers of that color held by all players are lost. This can dramatically shift the game's balance.

### Limited Scoring Tokens
With only 3 scoring tokens per game, deciding when and which civilization to score is the game's central strategic challenge. Spent tokens cannot be recovered.

### Continent Splitting
If tile placement splits a continent, the civilization may divide. The disc stays with the larger portion, and a new civilization is formed on the smaller portion.

### Maximum Followers
There is a limited supply of follower cubes for each color. If a color runs out, no more followers of that civilization can be collected.

### 9th Epoch Tile
The 9th Epoch tile triggers the final scoring if the 8th has already been drawn. The game can end at varying points depending on when Epoch tiles appear.

**Epoch Tile:** All players score 1 pt per pyramid. 8th Epoch = game ends.
