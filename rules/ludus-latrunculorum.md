## Overview

Ludus Latrunculorum ("game of brigands/soldiers") is a reconstructed ancient Roman strategy game for two players. Players manoeuvre pieces on a grid, capturing opponents by surrounding them. The goal is to capture all opponent pieces or render them immobile. Rules are reconstructed from historical fragments.

## Components

- 1 Board (typically 8x8 or 8x12 grid)
- 16 pieces per player (distinguishable by colour)

## Setup

Two players arrange 16 pieces each in two rows facing each other on opposite sides of the board.

## Turn Structure

Players alternate turns. On your turn, move one piece.

## Actions

### Movement
- Pieces move **orthogonally** (horizontally or vertically) any number of unobstructed spaces — similar to a rook in chess.
- Pieces cannot move diagonally.
- Pieces cannot jump over other pieces.

### Capture
- **Custodian capture:** A piece is captured when it becomes trapped between two enemy pieces on opposite sides along a row or column.
- Capture only occurs when the trapping player **actively moves** a piece to complete the trap.
- A piece may safely **move between** two enemy pieces without being captured — capture requires the opponent's active move.
- Multiple pieces can be captured in a single move if the moving piece creates multiple custodian captures simultaneously.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when one player:
- Captures all opponent's pieces, OR
- Immobilizes all remaining opponent's pieces (no legal moves)

That player wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Safe movement:** You may voluntarily move a piece between two enemy pieces without being captured. Only the moving player's action triggers captures.
- **Edge captures:** Board edges can serve as one side of a custodian trap in some rule variants.
- **Stalemate:** If neither player can make progress, the game may be declared a draw.
- **Historical uncertainty:** The exact ancient rules are not fully known; modern reconstructions vary. The most widely accepted version uses custodian capture on an 8x8 grid.
- **Dux variant:** Some versions include a special commander piece (Dux) with enhanced movement.

## Player Reference

**Movement:** Orthogonal, any distance, no jumping

**Capture:** Surround one enemy piece between two of yours along a line (horizontal or vertical)

**Win:** Capture all enemy pieces or immobilize them
