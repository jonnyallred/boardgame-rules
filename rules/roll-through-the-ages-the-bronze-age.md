## Overview

Roll Through the Ages: The Bronze Age is a civilization-building dice game designed by Matt Leacock (designer of Pandemic), published by Eagle-Gryphon Games. Players roll dice to collect food, workers, goods, and coins, using them to feed cities, build monuments, and purchase developments. The game distills the civilization-building genre into a quick, dice-rolling format played simultaneously on personal pegboards.

## Components

- 7 custom dice (6 unique faces each)
- 4 wooden pegboards (one per player)
- Wooden pegs for tracking cities, goods, food, and disasters
- Score sheets / reference cards (one per player)
- Pencils

## Setup

1. Each player takes a pegboard and sets all pegs to their starting positions.
2. Each player takes a score sheet and pencil.
3. All players start with 3 cities (and therefore roll 3 dice).
4. Place remaining dice and pegs within reach.

## Turn Structure

Each player's turn consists of five steps:

1. **Roll Dice:** Roll one die per city you have built (starting with 3). You may re-roll any or all dice up to 2 additional times (3 total rolls), but dice showing skulls cannot be re-rolled.

2. **Collect Goods and Food:** Mark food and goods on your pegboard according to dice results.

3. **Feed Cities and Resolve Disasters:** You must feed each city 1 food. Unfed cities cause disasters (lose points). If you rolled 3+ skulls, a disaster also strikes (affects you and potentially other players depending on skull count).

4. **Build Cities and/or Monuments:** Spend workers (from dice) to build cities (gaining more dice in future turns) or monuments (earning victory points). Mark progress on the score sheet.

5. **Buy One Development:** Spend coins (from dice, lost at end of turn) and/or stored goods to purchase one development from the score sheet. Developments cost 10-60 resources and provide ongoing abilities or end-game points.

6. **Discard Excess Goods:** If you have more than 6 goods stored, discard down to 6.

## Actions

**Dice Faces:**
- **3 Food:** Feed your cities.
- **3 Workers:** Build cities or monuments.
- **2 Food or 2 Workers (choose one):** Flexible resource.
- **7 Coins:** Use immediately for development purchases; lost at end of turn.
- **1 Good:** Stored on pegboard for future use.
- **2 Goods + 1 Skull:** Gain goods but add a skull. Skulls cannot be re-rolled.

**Building Cities:** Mark off worker boxes on a city row. When complete, gain 1 additional die from the next turn onward (max 7 cities/dice).

**Building Monuments:** Mark off worker boxes on a monument. Each monument has two point values: the first player to complete it earns the higher value; subsequent builders earn the lower value.

**Purchasing Developments:** Pay the listed cost (coins + goods). Each development can only be purchased once per player. Developments provide abilities like re-rolling dice, immunity to certain disasters, extra points, or resource bonuses.

**Disasters (Skulls):**
- 1-2 skulls: No effect.
- 3 skulls: Lose all your goods.
- 4 skulls: All other players lose 3 points.
- 5+ skulls: All other players lose 5 points AND you lose all goods.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends at the end of the round in which any player completes all available monuments OR purchases their 5th development. All players complete that round.

**Final Scoring:**
- Monument points (first/second builder bonuses)
- Development points
- Bonus points from specific developments
- Subtract disaster penalties

The player with the highest total score wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Skulls are locked after rolling and cannot be re-rolled.
- Coins must be spent immediately; they do not carry over between turns.
- Goods are stored on the pegboard and persist between turns (max 6).
- Some developments provide ongoing benefits that modify future turns (e.g., Irrigation lets you re-roll drought dice).
- The game can be played solo, competing against your own best score.
- Disasters triggered by skulls can harm other players, adding indirect interaction.
- Food deficit: each unfed city causes -1 point on your score.

## Player Reference

| Dice Face | Provides |
|---|---|
| 3 Food | 3 food |
| 3 Workers | 3 workers |
| Food/Workers | 2 food OR 2 workers |
| 7 Coins | 7 coins (use now or lose) |
| 1 Good | 1 storable good |
| 2 Goods + Skull | 2 goods, 1 skull (locked) |

**Turn:** Roll (up to 3 times) > Collect > Feed/Disasters > Build > Buy Development > Discard excess goods.

**Game end trigger:** All monuments built OR 5 developments purchased by any player.
