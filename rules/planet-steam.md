## Overview

Planet Steam is an economic resource extraction game where players act as entrepreneurs on a colony planet, trying to accumulate the most wealth. Players stake claims, deploy tanks and converters to extract resources (quartz, ore, water, energy), and buy and sell those resources in a volatile market driven by supply and demand. The player with the most total wealth (credits + resource value) at game end wins.

## Components

- 1 game board
- 90 plastic pieces: 35 bronze tanks, 14 silver quartz converters, 14 gold ore converters, 14 gray energy converters, 13 bronze superchargers
- 350+ cardboard pieces: 85 claim markers (5 colors + gray), resource tokens (quartz, ore, water, energy), credit tokens (1/5/10/20/50/100)
- 8 resource supply and price indicators
- 1 tank supply marker, 1 support airship marker
- 1 round marker, 4 order markers
- 67 cards: 10 specialist cards, 40 carrier cards, 14 certificate cards, 3 optional cards
- 5 reference sheets, 1 six-sided die

## Setup

1. Place game board centrally. Set up resource market with starting prices.
2. Each player takes claim markers, carrier cards, credits, and a reference sheet.
3. Deal specialist cards for initial player order.
4. Place tanks in the tank market at starting prices.
5. Set round marker to round 1.

## Turn Structure

Each round consists of multiple phases:

### 1. Specialist Selection
Players select specialist cards which determine turn order and grant special abilities for the round.

### 2. Claim Zones
In turn order, players stake claims on resource zones on the map.

### 3. Purchase Equipment
Players buy tanks and converters from the market. Tank prices fluctuate based on supply. Converters transform base resources into more valuable ones.

### 4. Deploy Equipment
Place purchased tanks and converters on claimed zones.

### 5. Resource Extraction
Tanks and converters on claimed zones produce resources based on the zone type and equipment.

### 6. Market Trading
Players buy and sell resources on the volatile market. Prices adjust dynamically based on supply and demand. The order in which players trade matters significantly.

### 7. Support Airship
The support airship provides additional extraction opportunities for the row it occupies.

## Actions

- **Stake Claims:** Place claim markers on resource zones to control extraction.
- **Buy Tanks:** Purchase extraction tanks (prices fluctuate with supply).
- **Buy Converters:** Acquire converters (quartz, ore, or energy) to transform resources.
- **Deploy Equipment:** Place tanks/converters on your claimed zones.
- **Trade Resources:** Buy/sell on the open market at current prices.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game lasts a set number of rounds. At game end, the player with the most total wealth wins.

**Wealth = Credits held + Market value of all resources owned**

Resource values are determined by their current market price at game end.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Market prices are volatile: buying raises prices, selling lowers them.
- Specialist cards provide unique abilities and determine turn order each round.
- Converters allow transforming cheaper resources into more expensive ones.
- The support airship bonus row changes position each round.
- Building licenses and luxury quarters (certificate cards) provide additional bonuses.
- Tank market supply is limited; prices rise as tanks are purchased.
- Player order during trading phase significantly impacts final wealth.
