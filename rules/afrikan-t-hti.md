## Overview

Afrikan tahti (The Star of Africa) is a classic Finnish board game first published in 1951. Players travel across a map of Africa by land, sea, and air, searching cities for hidden tokens. The goal is to find the Star of Africa — a legendary diamond — and return it to one of the two starting cities (Cairo or Tangier) before any other player.

## Components

- 1 game board (map of Africa with cities connected by routes)
- Tokens (placed face-down on cities): Star of Africa, gemstones (various values), horseshoe/visa tokens, robber tokens, and blank tokens
- Money bills (denominations of 1000, 5000, and 10000)
- Player pawns (one per player)
- 1 six-sided die

## Setup

1. Shuffle all tokens and place them face-down, one on each marked city on the map.
2. Each player chooses a pawn and receives 5000 in starting money from the bank.
3. All players place their pawns on one of the two starting cities: Cairo or Tangier.
4. Determine first player by any agreed method.

## Turn Structure

On each turn, a player:

1. **Rolls the die** and moves their pawn along connected routes up to the number shown. A player may stop at any city along the route even if they have remaining movement.
2. **Investigates a token** (if on a city with a face-down token): The player chooses one of two methods:
   - **Pay 1000 to the bank** and turn the token over immediately.
   - **Roll the die**: on a result of 4, 5, or 6, turn the token over for free; on 1, 2, or 3, the token remains face-down and the turn ends.
3. **Resolve the token** according to its type (see Actions below).

## Actions

### Token Types

| Token | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Star of Africa** | Take it — you must now race to Cairo or Tangier to win |
| **Gemstones (red)** | Collect 5000 from the bank |
| **Gemstones (yellow)** | Collect 3000 from the bank |
| **Gemstones (green)** | Collect 1000 from the bank |
| **Horseshoe / Visa** | If the Star has already been found by another player, take this token — you can win by reaching a starting city before the Star carrier. If the Star has not been found, the visa has no value and cannot be kept |
| **Robber** | Lose all your money; return it to the bank |
| **Blank (black)** | Nothing happens |

### Transportation

- **Land travel:** Roll the die and move along land routes. Free.
- **Sea travel:** Costs 1000 when departing a port city by sea. Roll the die each turn to move along sea routes.
- **Air travel:** Costs 3000. Fly directly between any two cities connected by air routes without rolling the die.

### Special Locations

- **Golden Coast:** If you find a gemstone here, you receive double its normal value.
- **Cape Town:** The first player to reach Cape Town receives a 5000 bonus from the bank.
- **Blue-circled dots:** If you land on one of these spots, you are stuck for one round (lose your next turn).
- **Islands:** If you are robbed while on an island, you are stuck for one round, then may take a boat to leave.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when a player carrying the Star of Africa (or a horseshoe/visa, once the Star has been found) reaches Cairo or Tangier. That player wins.

- **Primary win:** Find the Star of Africa and return to Cairo or Tangier.
- **Alternative win:** After another player has found the Star, find a horseshoe/visa token and reach Cairo or Tangier before the Star carrier.

If a player runs out of money, they cannot use paid transportation or pay to reveal tokens — they must rely on free die rolls to reveal tokens and can only travel by land.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- A player may not keep a horseshoe/visa unless the Star of Africa has already been found by someone else.
- The 2005 official rule amendment eliminated the need for sea travel tickets, allowing free movement across water to prevent unwinnable scenarios for cash-poor players. Check your edition for which sea travel rules apply.
- Tokens, once revealed, remain face-up for all players to see.
- There is no limit to the number of players that can occupy the same city.
