## Overview

Masterpiece: The Art Auction Game is a board game where players buy and sell famous paintings, trying to build the largest fortune in cash and valuable art. Each painting has a hidden value card that only the owner can see. Through auctions, trades, and strategic buying/selling, players try to acquire valuable paintings while avoiding forgeries.

## Components

- 1 circular game board
- Painting cards (famous artworks)
- Value cards (hidden values including some forgeries)
- Play money ($1.5M per player in various denominations)
- Player pieces
- Dice
- Rules booklet

## Setup

1. Each player receives $1,500,000 in play money.
2. Each player draws 1 painting and 1 value card (clipped to the painting's back, hidden from others).
3. Place remaining painting and value card decks on the board.
4. Determine first player by dice roll.

## Turn Structure

On your turn:

1. Roll the die and move clockwise.
2. Resolve the space you land on.

## Actions

### Board Spaces

| Space | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Bank Auction** | Top painting is auctioned; all players bid. Winner sees the value card. |
| **Private Auction** | One of your paintings goes to auction (chosen by player to your left). Other players bid; you receive payment. |
| **Buy from Bank** | Purchase the top painting for a fixed price, or decline (triggers auction). |
| **Sell to Bank** | Sell any painting for its hidden value (forgeries are worthless). |
| **Collect $3M or Value Card** | Choose: take $3M cash, or take a value card to attach to an unvalued painting you own. |
| **Buy from Player** | Pay exactly $3.5M for any opponent's painting. |
| **Inherit** | Receive a free painting + value card from the bank. |
| **Forced Sale** | Must sell one painting to the bank for $3.5M (even if worth more). |

### Auction Rules

- Minimum opening bid: $1,000,000
- Minimum raise: $500,000
- All players may bid.
- Bidding beyond your available cash incurs a $1M penalty or forfeits your next turn.
- No borrowing or private sales.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when all paintings and value cards have been drawn. Each player adds up:
- Cash on hand
- Value of owned paintings (from hidden value cards)

The player with the greatest total assets wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Forgeries:** Some value cards mark paintings as forgeries (worth $0). Players with forgeries want to sell them before the game ends.
- **Hidden values:** Only the painting's owner knows its value. Bluffing about a painting's worth is encouraged.
- Players cannot look at value cards of paintings they don't own.
- If a painting with no value card is sold to the bank, it is worth $0.
- Doubles on the die do not grant extra turns.

## Player Reference

| Auction Type | Who Bids | Who Gets Paid |
|-------------|----------|---------------|
| Bank Auction | All players | Bank (winner pays) |
| Private Auction | All except owner | Owner receives payment |
