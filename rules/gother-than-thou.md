## Overview

Gother Than Thou is a card game of betrayal and fame set in the goth subculture. Players compete to be the first to accumulate 20 Goth Points while managing negative conditions like Sickness and Debt. Each turn, players distribute cards from their hand to every player's Fate pile, creating a tense game of social strategy where you help yourself while burdening your opponents.

## Components

- 55-card deck
- Instructions

## Setup

1. A designated dealer shuffles the deck.
2. Deal 5 cards to each player.
3. Place the remaining cards face down in the center of the table as the draw pile.
4. Each player has an empty "Fate" pile area in front of them, face up.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, perform these steps in order:

### 1. Discard from Fate Pile
Discard one card from the top of your Fate pile (if you have any cards in it).

### 2. Distribute Cards
Place one card from your hand face up on top of every player's Fate pile, including your own. If you do not have enough cards for all players, play all remaining cards to the players of your choosing.

### 3. Draw Cards
Draw cards to replenish your hand up to your maximum hand size (5 minus your Sickness points). If you are NOT in debt, you may draw from either the draw pile or the discard pile. If you ARE in debt, you may only draw from the draw pile.

### 4. Pass
Play passes to the left.

## Actions

### Card Effects

Cards placed on Fate piles provide various effects to the player whose pile they sit on. The top card of each player's Fate pile is the active card that currently affects that player.

### Managing Scores

Players track three scores:

- **Goth Points:** Accumulated from cards. Reaching 20 wins the game.
- **Sickness:** Reduces maximum hand size by 1 per point. Cannot go below zero. Reaching 5 or more Sickness causes a "Swoon" (see Special Rules).
- **Money:** Tracks financial status. Negative money means you are in Debt, which restricts your drawing options.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The first player to have **20 or more Goth Points** at the end of their turn wins the game.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Swoon:** If a player reaches 5 or more Sickness points, they must immediately discard all cards in their Fate pile. This is called a "Swoon."
- **Sickness minimum:** Sickness cannot drop below zero.
- **Debt restriction:** Players in debt (negative Money) cannot draw cards from the discard pile; they may only draw from the face-down draw pile.
- **Hand size reduction:** Base hand size is 5. For every point of Sickness, maximum hand size decreases by 1.
- **Excess hand size:** If your hand size exceeds your current maximum (due to gaining Sickness), you must immediately discard down to your maximum.
- **Empty draw pile:** When the draw pile is exhausted, shuffle the discard pile to create a new draw pile.
- **Mandatory distribution:** You must play a card to every player's Fate pile if you have enough cards. You can only choose recipients when you have fewer cards than players.
- **Strategic placement:** Since you choose which card goes to which player (including yourself), the distribution step is the core strategic decision of the game.

## Player Reference

**Hand size:** 5 minus Sickness points

**Win condition:** 20+ Goth Points at end of your turn

**Turn order:** Discard from Fate > Distribute cards to all Fate piles > Draw to hand limit

**Swoon:** Triggered at 5+ Sickness; discard all Fate pile cards

**Debt:** Negative Money; cannot draw from discard pile

**Card distribution:** 1 card to every player's Fate pile each turn (including your own)
