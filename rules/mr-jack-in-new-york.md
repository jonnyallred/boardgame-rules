# Mr. Jack in New York

## Overview

Mr. Jack in New York is a standalone sequel to Mr. Jack, set on Manhattan island. One player is Jack hiding among 8 suspects; the other is the Detective trying to unmask him. The game features a hex-based Manhattan board with new mechanics including informant tokens, gas lamps, and an exit system. The core deduction mechanism remains: after activating characters, Jack declares visible or invisible, and the Detective eliminates suspects.

## Components

- 1 game board (Manhattan island with hex grid and turn track)
- 8 double-sided character tokens (Suspect / Innocent)
- 8 character cards
- 8 alibi cards
- 1 witness card
- 1 round counter
- Gaslight tiles
- Informant tokens
- Police cordons/barricades

## Setup

1. Place the Manhattan board and set up characters in starting positions.
2. Place gaslights, informants, and barricades as indicated.
3. All 8 character tokens start Suspect side up.
4. Jack draws 1 alibi card (secret identity).
5. Set round counter to 1.

## Turn Structure

Follows the same structure as the original Mr. Jack:

1. **Character Selection**: 4 characters per round, selected alternately (1/2/1 split between Detective and Jack, alternating who starts on odd/even rounds).
2. **Character Activation**: Move and use special abilities.
3. **Witness Call**: Jack declares Visible or Invisible.
4. **Elimination**: Flip characters that contradict Jack's declaration to Innocent.

## Actions

### Visibility

- **Visible**: On a hex adjacent to a lit gaslight, or adjacent to another character.
- **Invisible**: Not lit and not adjacent to any character.

### New York-Specific Mechanics

- **Informant tokens**: Characters can pick up informants for special effects.
- **New character abilities**: Each of the 8 New York characters has unique powers different from the original game.
- **Manhattan exits**: Jack must reach specific edge hexes while invisible to escape.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Detective wins**: Only 1 suspect remains after a witness call.

**Jack wins**: Escapes Manhattan through an exit while invisible, OR survives through round 8 with multiple suspects remaining.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Compatible in spirit with the original Mr. Jack but fully standalone with different characters and board.
- The turn track determines who selects characters first each round.
- All original deduction principles apply: visibility determination, character flipping, and escape mechanics.
