## Overview

Djambi (The Chessboard of Machiavelli) is a political strategy game for 3-4 players on a 9x9 grid. Each player leads a political party with 9 unique pieces, each representing a political archetype. The goal is to eliminate all opposing leaders and seize absolute power through a combination of conflict, coalition, and betrayal.

## Components

- 1 game board (9x9 grid with a central Labyrinth square)
- 36 playing pieces (9 per player in 4 colors):
  - 1 Leader per player
  - 1 Assassin per player
  - 1 Reporter per player
  - 1 Necromobile per player
  - 1 Provocateur per player
  - 4 Militants per player

## Setup

Each player places their 9 pieces in their designated corner of the 9x9 board according to the standard starting arrangement.

## Turn Structure

Players take turns clockwise. On each turn, a player moves one of their pieces according to its movement rules.

## Actions

### Piece Types and Movements

**Movers (manipulate position of pieces):**

| Piece | Movement | Special Ability |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Provocateur | Moves like a chess queen (any distance, straight line) | Moves a living enemy piece to any empty square, then the Provocateur takes the vacated square |
| Necromobile | Moves like a chess queen | Moves a dead piece (corpse) to any empty square, then takes the vacated square |

**Killers (eliminate pieces):**

| Piece | Movement | Special Ability |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Assassin | Moves like a chess queen | Kills any piece it lands on; the corpse stays where the Assassin started |
| Militants | Move like a chess queen but only 1-2 squares | Kill any piece they land on; corpse stays where Militant started |
| Reporter | Moves like a chess queen | Kills any piece adjacent to its destination (chooses which); corpse stays where the victim was |
| Leader | Moves like a chess king (1 square any direction) | Kills any piece it lands on; corpse stays where Leader started |

### Key Mechanics

- **Corpses:** Killed pieces remain on the board as corpses (flipped over). Corpses block movement.
- **The Labyrinth (center square):** A Leader in the Labyrinth gains special powers and controls all pieces of eliminated players.
- **Killing a Leader:** When a Leader is killed, the killing player takes control of all remaining pieces of the dead Leader's party.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when only one Leader remains alive. That player wins.

Alternatively, a Leader occupying the Labyrinth who controls enough pieces to be unassailable is effectively the winner.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Labyrinth benefits:** A Leader in the Labyrinth can only be attacked from an adjacent square. The Leader in the Labyrinth controls pieces of any eliminated party.
- **Dead pieces remain on the board** as obstacles (corpses).
- **The Necromobile** is the only piece that can move corpses, clearing paths or creating blockades.
- **The Provocateur** does not kill -- it forcibly relocates living enemy pieces.
- **The Reporter** kills at range (adjacent to destination, not the landing square itself).
- **Three-player variant:** Uses a hostage mechanic for the fourth color's pieces.
- Movement follows straight lines (like chess queen for most pieces). Pieces cannot jump over other pieces or corpses.
