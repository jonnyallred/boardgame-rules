## Overview

ACHTUNG! is a party card game about creating absurd laws and rules for everyone at the table to follow. As players draw and play law cards, the number of active rules builds until it becomes impossible for anyone to track them all. When someone breaks a law and another player notices, they shout "ACHTUNG!" to penalize the offender. The last lawmaker standing wins.

## Components

- Deck of cards consisting of five distinct types:
  - Global Law cards
  - Directed Law cards
  - Amendment cards
  - Filibuster cards
  - Additional card types as specified in the game

## Setup

1. Shuffle all cards into a single draw deck.
2. Deal a starting hand of cards to each player (as specified by the rules).
3. Place the remaining deck face-down in the center as the draw pile.
4. Determine first player.

## Turn Structure

On each turn, a player:

1. **Draws a card** from the draw pile.
2. **Plays a card** from their hand, placing it face-up on the table and announcing its effect.
3. **All players must follow** any active laws currently in play.

Between and during turns, any player may shout "ACHTUNG!" if they catch another player breaking an active law.

## Actions

### Card Types

| Card Type | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Global Law** | Creates a new rule that every player at the table must follow (e.g., "speak in an accent," "tap the table before drawing") |
| **Directed Law** | Creates a rule that targets a single specific player |
| **Amendment** | Modifies the text or effect of a law card already on the table |
| **Filibuster** | Interrupts the main game for a mini-challenge; winner gains an advantage |

### Calling ACHTUNG!

- Any player may call "ACHTUNG!" at any time if they observe another player breaking an active law.
- The offending player must **discard a card** from their hand as a penalty.
- If a player loses all their cards, they are sent to the **Gulag** (see Special Rules).
- False accusations (calling ACHTUNG! when no law was broken) may carry a penalty.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- A player is eliminated when they lose all cards from their hand (sent to the Gulag).
- **The last player remaining** with cards in hand wins the game.
- Alternatively, a player in the Gulag can re-enter the game by catching another player breaking a law.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **The Gulag:** When a player loses all their cards, they are placed in the Gulag. They cannot play cards or have laws directed at them, but they can observe other players. If a Gulag player successfully catches someone breaking a law (shouts "ACHTUNG!" correctly), they re-enter the game.
- **Law accumulation:** Laws remain active for the entire game unless removed by an Amendment card. The growing pile of laws is the central challenge — remembering and following all of them simultaneously.
- **Simultaneous enforcement:** Multiple laws can apply at the same time, creating increasingly complex behavioral requirements.
- **Social deduction element:** Players must constantly observe others while managing their own compliance with all active laws.

## Player Reference

| Situation | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Caught breaking a law | Discard 1 card |
| Lose all cards | Go to the Gulag |
| In Gulag, catch a lawbreaker | Re-enter the game |
| Last player with cards | Win the game |

**Card types:** Global Laws (affect all) → Directed Laws (target one) → Amendments (modify laws) → Filibusters (interrupt for mini-game)
