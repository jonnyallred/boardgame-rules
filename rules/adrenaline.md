## Overview

Adrenaline is a first-person shooter on a tabletop. Players move around a grid arena, grab ammo and weapons, and shoot each other to score points. Combat is diceless — weapons have fixed effects. Getting shot makes you faster (adrenaline actions unlock). The player with the most points when all skulls are removed wins.

## Components

- 1 game board (2 double-sided pieces → 4 possible arenas)
- 5 player boards, action tiles, figures, 20 damage tokens each
- 21 weapon cards
- 24 powerup cards
- 36 ammo tiles
- 3 ammo colors: red, yellow, blue (3 cubes of each per player)
- 1 killshot track with 5–8 skulls
- 1 double-sided board for Domination / Turret Mode
- 1 bot card
- Point tokens

## Setup

1. Choose two sides of the board pieces to form the arena (3–4 player: specific recommendations printed on board).
2. Place **5–8 skulls** on the killshot track (5 for first game; 8 for standard).
3. Shuffle powerup deck and weapon deck; place face-down on board.
4. Deal **3 weapons face-up** to each spawnpoint (red, yellow, blue squares).
5. Place ammo tiles face-down; deal one to each non-spawnpoint square.
6. Each player takes 3 cubes of each color; place 1 of each color in their ammo box.
7. Choose starting player.

**First turn — spawn:** Draw 2 powerup cards, keep 1, reveal and discard the other. Place figure on that card's spawnpoint color. Then take a normal turn.

## Turn Structure

Each turn, take **2 actions** in any order (can repeat the same action):

## Actions

**Run Around (Move)**
- Move 1, 2, or 3 squares.
- Moves are orthogonal only; can pass through doors, not walls.

**Grab Stuff** (free move included)
- Option A: Move 1 square, grab stuff in new square.
- Option B: Stay in current square, grab there.
- Cannot grab then move.
- *Non-spawnpoint:* Take the ammo tile; move depicted cubes to ammo box (max 3 per color); if tile shows a powerup card, draw one (max 3 powerups).
- *Spawnpoint:* Grab a weapon — choose 1 of 3 available, pay the cost (top cube of the weapon cost is already paid; pay the rest from ammo box or discard powerup cards). Weapon goes into hand loaded. Max 3 weapons; if at 3 must drop one into the now-empty spawnpoint slot.

**Shoot People**
- Play a weapon card from hand (card goes face-up in front of you — now unloaded).
- Declare targets (positions matter; targeting rules are in the Weapons Manual).
- Pay any additional costs for optional effects.
- Place damage tokens on target's board, starting from space 1.
- A weapon card stays unloaded until reloaded at end of turn.

### Adrenaline Actions (bonus moves when damaged)

| Damage on board | Bonus |
|---|---|
| 0–2 | Normal actions |
| 3–5 | Grab Stuff: move up to 2 squares before grabbing |
| 6–10 | Shoot People: move 1 square before shooting (in addition to above) |

Extra move is optional; still cannot grab-then-move or shoot-then-move.

### End of Turn

1. **Reload** (optional): Pay full reload cost (all cubes in upper-left corner of card) using cubes and/or powerup cards. Reload as many weapons as desired.
2. **Score** all boards that received a killshot this turn.
3. **Replenish**: Replace taken ammo tiles from stacks (shuffle discards if stacks empty); replace taken weapons from deck (if deck runs out, no new weapons appear).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Killshot & Scoring

- **11th damage token** = killshot. 12th = overkill.
- Overkill: victim gives attacker a revenge mark.
- At end of active player's turn, score all boards with killshots:
  - **First blood** (first player to deal damage): 1 point.
  - **Most damage**: 8 pts. Second: 6 pts. Third: 4 pts. Fourth: 2 pts. (Tie: player whose damage token landed first wins.)
  - Only players who dealt damage score points; minimum 1 point each.
  - **Killshot token**: taken from 11th space, placed on killshot track (leftmost skull position).
  - **Overkill token**: placed on same killshot track space as the killshot token.
  - Skull from killshot track goes to the dead board's 8-point pentagon (reduces future scoring).

**Diminishing values**: Each skull on a board reduces the top reward by 2. 1st death: 8-6-4-2. 2nd: 6-4-2-1. 3rd: 4-2-1-1. 4th+: 2-1-1-1.

**Double kill bonus**: Killing multiple opponents in one turn = +1 point.

### Death & Respawn

1. Tip figure over at killshot.
2. After active player's turn: score board, remove all damage tokens.
3. Draw 1 powerup card (can temporarily exceed 3).
4. Discard 1 powerup card; spawn on its color spawnpoint.
5. Marks remain. Weapons and ammo kept. Loaded stays loaded.

### Marks

- A mark is not damage — held above the damage track.
- When you next deal damage to a marked player, all your marks convert to damage simultaneously (apply damage first, then marks from the same action become new marks).
- Max 3 marks per player per color on any one board. Excess marks wasted.
- Marks survive respawn.

### Final Frenzy (standard game ending)

Triggered when last skull is removed from killshot track. Everyone (including the triggering player) gets one more turn.

**Frenzy setup:**
- All players with 0 damage flip their boards (2-1-1-1 side, no first blood point, skulls set aside, marks and ammo kept).
- All players flip their action tiles.

**Frenzy actions:**
- Before starting player (or if you are the starting player): **2 actions** — either (move ≤2 squares + optional reload + shoot) OR (move ≤3 squares + grab), plus move ≤4, plus move ≤2 + grab.
- After starting player: **1 action** — move ≤1 square + optional reload + shoot.

**Frenzy scoring:**
- After all final turns: score all boards with damage (even without killshots). Flipped boards give no first blood point.
- Score **killshot track**: most tokens = 8 pts, second = 6 pts, etc. Tie: earlier killshot wins.
- Player with most total points wins.
- **Tiebreaker**: higher killshot track score wins; if neither scored on killshot track, they remain tied.

### Sudden Death (5-skull variant)
Game ends when last skull is taken. Skip Final Frenzy. Score remaining boards normally, then score killshot track.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Ammo limits**: Max 3 cubes of each color. Powerup cards can substitute for ammo cubes when paying costs (discard a card showing the required color).
- **Weapon limits**: Max 3 weapons in hand. Dropping a weapon places it at the spawnpoint slot as partially loaded (anyone can grab it for the standard cost).
- **Powerup limit**: Max 3 in hand, except briefly during respawn draw.
- **Overkill**: Any damage over 12 is wasted; hand back excess marks if marks would push past 12.
- **You can die on your own turn**: Take your second action and reload before your board is scored. A self-kill does not count toward double kill.
- **Teleporter**: Triggers only the turret you land on (not passed-through turrets).
- **Final frenzy multiple killshots**: If you trigger final frenzy by dealing multiple killshots, all killshot/overkill tokens go to the track. No need to determine which gets the final skull.
- **Powerup cards as ammo**: Can pay all or part of any ammo cost by discarding a powerup showing the required cube color.

## Game Modes

### Domination Mode
Cover killshot track with domination board; place 8 skulls.

- Beginning on your **second turn**, you can tag spawnpoints (in addition to damaging players).
- **Tag methods**: (1) Deal damage to the spawnpoint (only 1 token even if weapon does more; once per turn), OR (2) end your turn as the sole figure on the spawnpoint.
- Spawnpoint damage tracking: put your token on leftmost empty space of the spawnpoint track.
- **Spawnpoint strikes back**: If you end your turn on a spawnpoint, it deals you 1 damage (your own color). Those points go to the bank (no one scores them).
- Killshots and overkill are not tracked on the killshot track.
- Overkill tokens go on any spawnpoint track of your choice.
- **Final frenzy trigger**: Last skull taken OR two spawnpoint tracks each have ≥8 tokens.
- **Spawnpoint track scoring** (end of game only): 8-6-4-2-1. Ties all get the reward (e.g., two tied for first both get 8; next player gets 4).
- Tractor Beam and Vortex Cannon can tag spawnpoints (only if visible). Area weapons tag spawnpoints. T.H.O.R. can target a spawnpoint as first/second/third target but cannot chain from it.

### Turret Mode
Cover killshot track with turret board; place 8 skulls.

- No ammo tiles on the board. Instead, 5 face-up ammo tiles on turret board; replenish at end of each turn.
- Squares that normally offer ammo now also have turrets.
- **Grab ammo**: Choose a tile from the turret board (not the board square).
- **Commandeer a turret**: When you grab ammo on a turret square, optionally skip one ammo/powerup and take control of the turret instead. Place your token on it; any existing token goes to your marks (max 3).
- **Entering a turret square**: If the turret belongs to another player, that player deals you 1 damage. Multiple moves in one action each trigger separately.
- Turret damage does not trigger marks, cannot be boosted by Targeting Scope, and cannot be responded to with Tagback Grenade.
- Leaving or starting on a turret square has no effect. Teleporter: only triggers turret you land on.
- Killshots/overkill are not tracked.
- **Turret control scoring** (end of game): Most turrets = 8 pts, then 6, 4, 2, 1. Tiebreaker: final turn came earlier.

### Adding a Bot (3–5 player)
- Use for 3-player games (or optionally 4-player) to add more targets.
- Bot has a player board and damage tokens; no ammo or action tile.
- Bot card given to second player and passed around.
- **Spawn**: Starting player places bot at any spawnpoint.
- **Bot action** (on second player's turn and after): Taken alongside your 2 normal actions (any order). Move bot 1 square (optional), then if bot has any visible targets (not you, not the bot), must choose 1 and deal **1 damage** in bot's color. If bot has both adrenaline actions unlocked, shot also gives 1 mark.
- In Domination Mode, bot can target visible spawnpoints.
- **Powerup interactions**: Targeting Scope cannot boost bot action. Teleporter cannot move bot. Newton can move bot (even before bot action). Tagback Grenade gives mark to bot if played in response to bot damage.
- Bot is scored exactly like a player. You get double kill bonus if bot and another are killed in your turn.

## Player Reference

**On Your Turn:**
1. Take 2 actions (Run Around / Grab Stuff / Shoot People) in any order.
2. Reload any weapons (pay full upper-left cost in cubes/powerups).
3. Score killshots.
4. Replenish board.

**Weapon Cost**: Upper-left corner = grab cost; top cube already paid, pay rest. Same cubes = reload cost.

**Adrenaline**: 3+ damage → Grab up to 2 moves. 6+ damage → Shoot with 1 extra move.

**Scoring**: Damage = 8/6/4/2 (diminishes with each death). First blood = 1. Killshot track at end = 8/6/4/2/1.

**Marks**: Convert to damage next time you deal damage to that player. Max 3 per player.
