## Overview

Poison is a card game where players take turns playing Potion Cards into cauldrons, trying to avoid pushing the total value in any cauldron over 13. If you cause a cauldron to exceed 13, you must collect all previous cards from it. At the end of the round, each collected card is worth negative points, but collecting the most of a single color grants immunity to that color. Poison Cards are wild and worth double penalty points.

## Components

- 3 Cauldron boards
- 50 cards:
  - 14 Potion Cards in each of 3 colors (42 total): 2 cards valued at 4, 3 cards each valued at 1, 2, 5, and 7
  - 8 Poison Cards valued at 4 each

## Setup

1. Place the 3 cauldron boards in the center of the table.
2. Choose a dealer. The dealer shuffles and deals all 50 cards one at a time to all players. Some players may receive one more card than others.
3. With exactly 3 players, deal 4 hands and remove the extra hand from the game unseen.
4. The dealer rotates to the left each round.

## Turn Structure

The player to the left of the dealer goes first. Play proceeds clockwise. On your turn, you must play exactly one card face-up into a cauldron.

## Actions

### Playing a Potion Card
- Each Potion Card must be played into the cauldron matching its color.
- Potion Cards of the same color may not be in more than one cauldron at a time.

### Playing a Poison Card
- Poison Cards (value 4) may be played into any cauldron.
- If a cauldron contains only Poison Cards, its color is not yet determined. The first Potion Card added determines the cauldron's color, but it must not match any other existing cauldron's color.

### Exceeding 13
- If the card you play causes the total value of cards in a cauldron to exceed 13, you must take all previous cards in that cauldron and place them face-down in front of you. The card you just played remains in the cauldron as the start of a new pile.
- You may not look at your collected cards until the end of the round.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

At the end of a round (when all cards have been played):

1. Each player examines their collected cards.
2. For each of the 3 Potion Card colors, the player who collected the most cards of that color discards all cards of that color (immunity). In case of a tie for most, no one gets immunity for that color.
3. Each remaining Potion Card in your collection scores 1 penalty point.
4. Each Poison Card in your collection scores 2 penalty points.

Play multiple rounds. The player with the fewest total penalty points after the agreed number of rounds wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- With 3 players, always deal a 4th dummy hand that is removed from play.
- Potion Cards of the same color can only exist in one cauldron at a time.
- If a cauldron has only Poison Cards, the next Potion Card played determines its color.
- Ties for most cards of a color mean no player gets immunity for that color.
- You cannot examine your face-down collected cards until the round ends.
