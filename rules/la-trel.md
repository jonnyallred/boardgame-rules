## Overview

La-Trel is an abstract strategy board game designed by Richard Morgan. Nominated for the Spiel des Jahres in 1996, it features chess-like pieces on a grid board where attackers capture by jumping (as in checkers) and defenders protect by blocking. The goal is to defeat all of your opponent's attacker pieces.

## Components

- 1 game board (grid)
- Per player (16 pieces each):
  - 8 Defenders (placed in pawn positions)
  - 4 Sabres (attacker pieces)
  - 2 Tridents (attacker pieces)
  - 2 Warriors (attacker pieces)

## Setup

Each player places their 16 pieces on their side of the board in a chess-like formation:
- 8 Defenders line up in the second row (pawn positions).
- Sabres, Tridents, and Warriors line up along the back rank.

## Turn Structure

Players alternate turns. On your turn, move one of your pieces according to its movement rules. If a capture is available, it must be taken (captures are mandatory, as in checkers).

## Actions

### Piece Movement

| Piece | Movement | Capture |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Defender | 1 square in any direction except diagonally | Cannot capture |
| Warrior | Any number of squares in any direction (like Chess Queen) | Jumps over opponent's piece |
| Sabre | Any number of squares cardinally (like Chess Rook) | Jumps over opponent's piece |
| Trident | Any number of squares diagonally (like Chess Bishop) | Jumps over opponent's piece |

### Capturing
- Attacker pieces (Warriors, Sabres, Tridents) capture by jumping over an opponent's piece to land on the empty space beyond (as in checkers).
- Multiple captures in a single turn are possible (chain jumps).
- Defenders cannot capture.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- **Win condition**: Defeat (capture) all of your opponent's attacker pieces (Warriors, Sabres, and Tridents).
- Defenders do not count for victory -- only attackers matter.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Mandatory captures**: If a capture is available, it must be taken.
- **Defenders are invulnerable targets**: They cannot be captured, but they also cannot capture. They serve as blockers.
- **Chain jumps**: Multiple captures in a single turn are allowed if the jumping piece can continue jumping.
- **Movement range**: Warriors, Sabres, and Tridents can move any number of squares in their allowed directions, but capture still requires jumping exactly one space over the target.
- **No promotion**: Unlike chess, pieces do not promote.

## Player Reference

**Pieces per Player:**

| Piece | Count | Movement | Can Capture? |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Defender | 8 | 1 space, non-diagonal | No |
| Warrior | 2 | Any direction, any distance | Yes (jump) |
| Sabre | 4 | Cardinal only, any distance | Yes (jump) |
| Trident | 2 | Diagonal only, any distance | Yes (jump) |

**Win:** Capture all 8 of opponent's attacker pieces (4 Sabres + 2 Tridents + 2 Warriors).
