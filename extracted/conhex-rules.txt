A strategy board game for 2 players by 
Michail Antonow 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
ConHex is an abstract strategy game for 
two players, ‘Yellow’ and ‘Red’. Both aim to 
complete a contiguous chain of connected cells 
between the 2 sides of their colour. Draws are 
not possible. 
 
Cells are claimed by ‘conquering’ them. In 
order to conquer a cell, players must surround 
it with pegs. 
 
MATERIAL 
 
A ConHex board with 41 cells and 69 
‘holes’. 
 
 
 
26 rectangles of each colour. 
26 pegs of each colour. 
Carrying case. 
 
GAME RULES 
 
Each player has an allocated colour. Red 
starts. The game is played in turns. 
 
On his first move, Yellow may elect to swap 
colours (swap option). This is to prevent overly 
strong opening moves. 
 
Each turn the current player places one of 
their pegs on an empty hole, which may 
conquer one or more cells. A cell is conquered 
by the first player to occupy at least half of its 
surrounding holes. A yellow rectangle is placed 
on cells conquered by Yellow, and a red 
rectangle is placed on cells conquered by Red. 
 
The central cell, which has five holes, is 
conquered by the player who has occupied 
any three of these holes. No rectangle is 
needed to be placed in this case. 
 
The first player that completes a contiguous 
chain of connected cells of his colour between 
the 2 sides of his colour wins the game. Ties 
are not possible. 
 
 
 
Example: Yellow has conquered the top left cell 
and Red has conquered the other three coloured 
cells. The lower red cell is surrounded by three 
pieces of each colour, so we know that Red must 
have placed their third piece there before Yellow 
did.
