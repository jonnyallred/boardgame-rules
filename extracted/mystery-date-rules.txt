The MYSTERY DOOR is the centerpiece of the gameboard. The knob 
opens the door to reveal one of the four DATES, or the DUD. 
They are as follows:
 
 • FORMAL DANCE DATE is dressed in a tuxedo and holding a 
 corsage box.
 • BOWLING DATE is dressed in a jacket and red sweater, and 
 carrying his bowling bag.
 • BEACH DATE is dressed in a blue sport shirt and trunks 
 and carries a beach umbrella, towel and picnic cooler.
 • SKIING DATE is dressed in skiing outfit and carries his 
 skis and poles.
 • THE PEST (DUD) is dressed so shabbily that no one will 
 go on a date with him!
 Rules 
THE FIRST TIME YOU PLAY
Punch the four cardboard “girl” movers out and place each one 
in a plastic mover base. 
Insert the Mystery Door into the gameboard, slide the tab on the right 
side of the door into the large slot on the board, and then push the 
bottom of the door hinges so they hook into the two small slots. 
THE OBJECT OF THE GAME is for the player to collect cards in 
order to be READY for the DATE that will appear when she 
OPENS THE DOOR.
EQUIPMENT
The BOARD–The playing spaces, arranged around the outside edge of 
the board, contain messages instructing the player how to proceed.

The CARDS–There are 48 “OUTFIT” cards of 4 colors, equally
divided, (pink, green, blue and orange) and numbered as 1’s, 2’s, 
and 3’s. They picture the proper outfits needed by the player for 
the 4 different DATES in the game.
A player is “READY” for the “Formal Dance Date” if she holds 
the 1, 2, and 3 PINK cards; the 1, 2, and 3 GREEN cards go with 
the “Bowling Date”; the 1, 2, and 3 BLUE cards go with the “Ski-
ing Date”; and the 1, 2, and 3 ORANGE cards go with the “Beach 
Date.”
There are four “GIRL” playing pieces on plastic stands, in four 
different colored housecoats.
There is 1 die (dice) which governs the moves of the players.
One player shuffles the cards and deals TWO, one at a time, to 
each player. The remaining cards are placed, face-down, on the 
board at one side of the DOOR to be used as a DRAW PILE. The 
top card of this Draw Pile is placed, face-up, at the other side of 
the DOOR, becoming the DISCARD PILE.
Each player chooses a “Girl” playing piece and places it on the 
board in the BLUE “OPEN THE DOOR” CORNER space nearest her 
TO START.
They take turns, starting to the left of the dealer. Each player, in 
turn, throws the die and moves her playing piece, CLOCKWISE, 
around the board from space to space according to the number 
thrown. For example, if a player throws a “3”, she moves her 
playing piece 3 spaces. Two or more pieces may stop on the 
same space. Players ALWAYS MOVE CLOCKWISE AROUND THE 
BOARD.
Each space a player STOPS ON gives her instructions as follows:
A.In the “TAKE” spaces, the player takes the cards as directed,
 from the top of the pile, and adds them to her hand–UNTIL SHE
 HOLDS SIX CARDS. Whenever a “TAKE” space brings her hand
 to OVER SIX, she MUST discard enough cards to bring her hand
 down to six. These cards are placed face-up on the 
 DISCARD PILE.
 For example: If the player has 5 cards in her hand, and lands on
 the “Take 2 Cards from Draw Pile” space, she must draw 2 cards
 from the top of the Draw Pile and then discard one card OF HER
 CHOICE, leaving her hand with 6 cards at the end of the turn.

RULES

B.In the “SWAP” spaces, the player swaps a card, as directed (with 
 the player to the “right”, “left” or “any player”). To SWAP, the player 
 FIRST places ANY card she chooses from her hand, facedown, in
 front of the player she is to swap with. Then she draws one card 
 from this opponent’s hand who must hold her cards with their backs
 to her so the swapping player cannot see which card she is drawing.
 She must add this card to her hand, while the OPPONENT adds the
 “face-down” card to her hand.
C.In the “TAKE 1 CARD FROM EITHER DRAW OR DISCARD PILE”
 spaces, the player has a choice. If the player chooses, she may
 take the top card from the DISCARD PILE, knowing the card, or draw
 the card from the DRAW PILE.
Whenever the DISCARD PILE is used up, take the top card from the 
DRAW PILE to start a new one. When the DRAW PILE is used up, 
shuffle the discards (leaving the top card) to start a new DRAW PILE.
When a player stops on an “OPEN THE DOOR” space and she has a 
complete outfit for a “Date” (that is, the 1, 2 and 3 cards of ANY ONE 
COLOR), she is “READY” and opens the door. But if she does NOT 
have a complete 3-card outfit of one color, she does NOTHING in 
that turn.
TO OPEN THE DOOR, a player must show her 3-card outfit to the 
opponents. Then she makes sure the door is COMPLETELY CLOSED. 
Then she TURNS and SPINS the doorknob and SLOWLY opens the door 
wide to disclose which “DATE” has arrived.
If the “Date” seen when the DOOR is opened is NOT the correct one 
to go with the 3 “outfit” cards shown, the player must end her turn by 
closing the door COMPLTELEY and SPINNING THE KNOB to make it 
impossible for the following player to know which date will next appear.
If the player opening the door finds the “DUD”, she must discard the 
3-outfit cards she has shown onto the Discard pile and immediately 
draw 3 more cards from the Draw Pile to end her turn.
 Note: It is possible that a player might be READY for two “Dates” 
 at the same time. For example, she might be holding the 1, 2 and
 3 GREEN cards and the 1, 2 and 3 BLUE cards. In this case she
 must choose which of the “Dates” she wishes to find and place
 these 3 cards face up on the table. The other 3-card sequence she
 holds in her hand for future play.
Players continue to move around the board (clockwise), taking, 
swapping, discarding and opening the door according to the 
rules until one is the winner.
When a player opens the door and finds the “Date” for which she is 
READY (she has the 1, 2 and 3 cards of the same color which apply
to this date), she has WON THE GAME.
