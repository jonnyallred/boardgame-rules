Bagh Chal comes from Nepal and Bagh Bandi comes from Sri Lanka. Depending on where 
the game is played, the pawns can symbolize tigers, leopards, goats, cows or dogs. Bagh Chal
and Bagh Bandi are hunting games where the power and number of the pawns varies 
between the players.

MATERIALS
PROGRESS OF THE GAMES
-1 board divided into 4 blocks (each block is divided into 8 perpendicular triangles)
-4 tiger pawns (2 tiger pawns in Bagh Bandi)
-20 goat pawns
Object of the game
For the player who controls the tiger pawns: To eat all the goats
For the player who controls the goat pawns: To immobilize the tigers while avoiding being eaten
For Bagh Bandi: Place the tiger and goat pawns on the board as in Figure No.1. The 20 goat
pawns are placed in stacks of 4 or 5.
The player who controls the goat pawns makes the first move, removing a goat pawn from a
stack and placing it on an empty point on the board. In the next few rounds, this player can
either move a single goat pawn to another empty point on the board or remove another goat
pawn from a pile placing it on an empty point on the board. Only single goat pawns can move
across the board; the stacks of goat pawns do not move.
Tiger pawns eat goat pawns by jumping over them. A tiger pawn can either jump over and eat
a single goat pawn or jump over a stack of goat pawns and eat the goat pawns on the top of
the stack.
Tiger pawns are trapped by goat pawns when there is no available point to jump to.
For Bagh Chal: Place the tiger and goat pawns on the board as in Figure No.2. Unlike Bagh
Bandi, there are 4 tiger pawns placed in the corners of board and the goat paws are not
stacked. Follow the rules of Bagh Bandi to play.
