In this game you play the commander of the worthy vessel 'HMS Dauntless' tasked by 
the Emperor of the Grand Compact to patrol the skies protecting against the threat of 
merciless Air Pirates. 
To win you must destroy a squadron of 6 Air Pirates, each represented by a die. You lose 
the game if 4 sections of your ship show the black crosses of overwhelming damage.
Items needed
6 Zeppelin Dice
6 Air Pirate Dice
1 Crew Die
1 D12
Zeppelin Dice:
Your zeppelin consists of 6 dice, numbered 1 through 6. There are 4 die faces for each 
ship section (with 2 faces on the ends showing a cross section through the ship), these 
ship sections will rotate as your ship takes on damage (indicated by explosion icons). As 
the dice faces rotate - attack, defense, repair and movement rolls will be harder to make.
Four of your ship sections (marked 1, 2, 4, and 5 in the upper right corner) are gun 
emplacements and are used to attack the Air Pirates. They are designated with a red 
circle and a number, small black squares designate gun ranges used during your attack.
One of your ship sections (marked 3) is the command deck and controls repairs. It is 
designated with a yellow circle and a number.
One of your ship sections (marked 6) is the engine and is used to move your ship. It is 
designated with a green circle and a number.

All ship sections also have a blue circle and a number, this is the defense value

During the course of the game you will be rolling a d12 and checking it against 
these colored circles with numbers. If the d12 value is higher than the value 
in the colored circle your roll is successful. As you will notice, when your ship 
starts to take on damage, these rolls are increasingly harder to make, though your 
crew members may offer a bonus to these rolls.
Air Pirates:
There are 6 types of Air Pirates you may encounter, these will be determined 
during the initial set up. Each Air Pirate has a red circle and number for its 
attack value as well as a set of small black hit point numbers surrounding it. 
Each Air Pirate also has a red triangle used for initial orientation.
Crew:
There are 6 types of crew members, each allows a particular bonus during a 
round, with the exception of the first round! Air Pirates always start off with a 
sneak attack surprising your crew as they come screaming out of the cloudy sky.
d12:
A 12 sided die is used to position the Air Pirates during set up and used to make 
attack, defense, movement and repair rolls.

Initial setup:
Place the 6 ship dice in a column with the 6 numbers in white boxes oriented towards 
the right, in order top to bottom (marked 1-6). This represents your zeppelin.
Roll the 6 Air Pirate dice to see what squadron of Air Pirates you will fight.
Next roll a d12 to position each Air Pirate die. There are 12 possible positions the 
Air Pirates can strike, 6 portside, 6 starboard.
The upper right side of zeppelin die 1 is position 1 and counting clockwise around 
the dice faces, the right side of zeppelin die 1 is position 12 (The nose of a zeppelin 
is heavily armoured against ice and lightning storms and the engines spit out boiling 
steam and red hot coal dust. Both make for poor targets for Air Pirates). Roll the 
d12 and position the Air Pirate dice, one by one with the red triangle facing your 
zeppelin. During this part of the setup several Air Pirates may be attacking from 
the same position. If this is the case stack the Air Pirate dice behind each other in a 
horizontal row. These positions will be important later for gun ranges.

Playing the Game:
A round consists of 5 phases:
1. Air Pirate attack and your defense
2. Air Pirate movement
3. Your movement or attack
4. Your repairs
5. Crew re-roll
1. Air Pirate attack and your defense
The villainous Air Pirates attack first during the round. Air Pirates always attack the 
section of the ship they are facing (to their left or right). Air Pirates stacked next 
each other also make an attack to the adjacent ship section. The Diplomat allows 
you to choose one Air Pirate who will not attack during this round.
Roll the d12 for each Air Pirate, going clockwise one by one around the zeppelin, 
and check the value against the attack number of the Air Pirate (the number inside 
the red circle) if it is lower than this red number or a tie it is not a successful attack. 
If it is higher, the attack is successful and you will roll a d12 again for the particular 
ship section needed to be defended. The steam welder allows a +1 bonus to this die 
roll or the able crew can add a +1 value to this roll if you choose not to use their 
bonus for attack or movement during phase 3. If the roll value is higher than the 
blue defense value your defenses hold and no damage is incurred. If the roll value 
is less than or equal to the blue defense number, your defense has failed. Rotate the 
die one quarter turn to the next die face (you can always check which face to rotate 
to by the number of explosions indicated. If one explosion is showing, rotate the 
die to 2 explosions, etc...) to show the new damage the ship has taken. If black 
crosses -        - are revealed on 4 ship sections, the game is over and you have lost.

3. Your movement or attack
You may now decide to move your ship or attack the Air Pirates. You may not do 
both during this phase unless indicated by the Lieutenant. 
Moving allows you to bring your guns into range or to fly damaged ship sections 
out of range of an Air Pirate's attack. You may not move beyond the adjacency of 
any Air Pirate die. 
If you decide to move, roll a d12 and if the value is higher than the number in the 
green circle on ship section 6 you may move your entire ship up or down one die 
position of your choice. The able crew can add a +1 value to this roll if you choose 
not to use their bonus for defense or attack.

If you decide to attack roll, a d12 for each gun emplacement (each gun emplacement 
has a set of black squares on ship sections 1, 2, 4 and 5, there will be 1, 2 or 3 
black squares). 
The set of black squares indicate the range of the guns. 3 squares means that gun 
has a range of 3 die spaces, either up and down along the sides of your zeppelin or 
outward to an Air Pirates die position. Each gun fires only once during a round but 
multiple guns may be used to attack the same Air Pirate target. The captain can add 
a +1 to your attack roll or the able crew can add a +1 value to this roll if you choose 
not to use their bonus for defense or movement. Compare this d12 value to the red 
number indicated on the ship section operating the gun to see if it is successful.
(In our example gun emplacement die 2 has a gun range of 3 on its starboard and 
port sides, this means it can hit an Air Pirate adjacent to die 1, 3 or 4, or a range 
outward to any stacked Air Pirates, so die 2 could hit the Air Pirate behind the one 
adjacent to die 1 as well.)
Port guns can only reach Air Pirates on the port side, and starboard guns can only 
reach those Air Pirates on the starboard side. As your ships takes on damage you 
will notice the gun range decreases and you may also lose a gun completely on one 
side of your ship.
If your attack is successful against an Air Pirate, roll a d12 and divide by 3, rounding 
down for your damage value for that gun's attack. Rotate the Air Pirate die clockwise 
to the next lowest hit point number, one point for each point of damage you have 
given to the Air Pirate. (Note when first positioning your enemy craft you will 
have oriented the red triangle so that it points towards your zeppelin, showing the 
highest hit point value). If the Air Pirate's hit points go below 1, that craft has been 
destroyed and your job has gotten a bit easier. If multiple Air Pirates are stacked 
adjacent to each other when this occurs, move the next Air Pirate so it is adjacent 
to your zeppelin.

4. Your repairs
You may now try and repair one damaged section of your ships.
Roll the d12 and if this value is higher than the number in the yellow circle on ship 
section 3 you may rotate a section of your ship of your choice up one damage level 
(i.e. 2 explosions to 1 explosion, etc..), repairing it. The engineer allows you to repair 
one additional ship section of your choice during this phase without having to roll 
for the repair number.
5. Crew re-roll
Roll the crew die to see what crew member can offer bonuses next round.
Repeat the phases of each round in order until you destroy 
all the Air Pirates or die valiantly in the attempt. Good luck!

Revenants
1a. Air Pirate Boarding
Revenants - rumored to be the souls and broken bodies of tortured Air Pirate prisoners trapped within 
automated fighting mannikins constructed of iron and brass - are boarding parties that attack ship 
sections, crippling their effectiveness until dealt with.
After a successful Air Pirate attack and subsequent first defense roll for damage, 
make a second defense roll. If the roll value is less than or equal to the blue defense 
number that ship section has been boarded by Revenants, villainous pirate raiding 
teams. Place a Revenant Marker on top of that ship section. Until the next Air 
Pirate attack round (phase 1) that section is busy repelling boarders and no 
attacks, movement or repairs rolls can be made by you for that section. Continue to 
phase 2: Air Pirate movement.
At the beginning of the next phase 1, remove all Revenant Markers.

Expansion 5
Air Pirate: The Brass Dagger
The rumours are true! Air Pirates have recently begun using a new super-ship - The Brass 
Dagger - to attack cities along the coastline of the Great Compact. Can the brave men and women 
our our Emperor’s Navy rise to the challenge?
Rules for using Expansion 5
Initial Setup:
Roll the d12 for the ship’s initial 1 to 12 position and place the first ship section 
with the red triangle facing your zeppelin at that position, followed by the other two 
sections in a column. The Brass Dagger is intended to be a close quarters broadsides 
strike-boarding ship. Any other Air Pirates will always stack outwards from it during 
initial setup as well as in Phase 2. This ship replaces two of the base game Air Pirate 
dice, unless you want a more challenging game - if so, feel free to use all the Air Pirates.
1. Air Pirate attack:
Each section of the ship makes one attack on your zeppelin as if it were an individual 
air pirate ship. Use the ship section’s red attack numbers for their attack rolls. Only 
sections touching your zeppelin may make an attack. Revenant expansion rules apply 
for each section touching your zeppelin during your defense roll as well.
2. Air Pirate movement: 
Roll the d12 as you normally would for Air Pirate movement, sliding the ship up or 
down one position or leaving it where it is. If the roll causes The Brass Dagger to move 
to the other side of your zeppelin use the first section of the ship for placement. If 
one or more Air Pirates are already occupying the spaces that will be taken up with 
the The Brass Dagger, stack them outwards from it.
3. Your attack:
Your zeppelin guns can attack any section of The Brass Dagger as long as you have 
range. If an attack is successful, instead of rolling for hit point damage, rotate The 
Brass Knife ship section to its next damage die face. Each successful attack by you 
on a section will rotate it only one face. It will take at least 6 successful attacks to 
defeat The Brass Knife. If two sections show pirate skulls you have destroyed the Air 
Pirate ship.
