Will you play it safe or take a chance and spin again
to try to win the most bananas? Look out for the monkey
with six bananas, it could be a game changer!
 
• 1 two-part 3D tree • 32 monkey cards • 1 pool board • 1 spinner board
• 1 two-part plastic spinner arrow
Cheeky Monkeys
Cheeky Monkeys
Setting up the game
Spread the monkey cards 
face down on the table.
Place the
spinner and
pool board 
within reach
of all players.
Each player chooses a coloured section 
of the 3D tree (red, blue, yellow or 
green). They then take one face down 
monkey card from the table and hook 
it onto their section.
Slot together the 3D tree by 
matching the coloured strips 
at the bottom to create four 
coloured sections.
Attach the spinner arrow to 
the spinner board.
Spin the spinner
1a
The youngest player starts 
by spinning the spinner.
If the arrow lands on a 
banana section, turn 
over a monkey card
and place it face up
in front of you.
Decide whether to end your turn by keeping the monkey 
and hooking it onto your section of the tree or take a 
chance and spin again to try to win more bananas. If you 
spin again and land on a banana section you can take 
another monkey card from the table, but if the arrow 
lands on...
...the cheeky monkey, you can take 
one monkey from another player’s 
section and hook it, together with any 
other monkeys collected on your turn, 
onto your section of the tree. Play 
passes to the next player.
Top tip: There is one monkey with 6 bananas!
...the pool monkey, take any monkeys 
you have collected on your turn and 
place them on the pool board. Play 
passes to the next player. 
If you have not collected any monkeys on your 
turn, play passes to the next player.
Your monkey will be holding 0, 1, 2, 3 or 6 bananas. 
If the spinner lands on a 
banana section, you can 
spin again as many 
times as you like until 
you decide to hook the 
monkeys onto your 
section of the tree or the 
arrow lands on another 
spinner section.
Remember not to hook 
your monkeys onto your 
section of the tree until 
your turn is over!
The winner is the player with the
most bananas. 
The winner!
The game ends when there are no 
face down monkey cards left on the 
table. N.B. The person playing at this point 
takes any monkeys collected on that turn
and hooks them onto their section of the tree.
End of the game
Players then take all the 
monkeys from their section of 
the tree and count how many 
bananas they have collected.
Any monkeys on the pool board at the 
end of the game are not counted.
